Investor Pulse: Takeaways from Buy-Side Discussions

Petrobras - We expect discussions on diesel ICMS tax to continue, following the release of guidelines for the diesel subvention program. Last Thursday, the government issued a decree with guidelines regarding reference prices for the diesel subvention program (see details on the recently published guidelines here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118858>  and details on the subvention program here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118668> ). The guidelines indicated that the diesel reference price for Petrobras to gain the R$0.32/liter diesel should be based on its current selling price of R$3.65/liter - this should allow Petrobras to capture R$3.97 for each liter of diesel sold. We believe some investors interpreted the decree as the government introducing a price cap to Petrobras' diesel price. However, we believe that (i) Petrobras remains free to adjust fuel prices as it deems necessary and (ii) the company could eventually raise diesel prices more than R$0.32/liter in order to boost diesel revenues/improve refining margins. For this week, we wait for ANP to publish the final details regarding the reference price formula, while we also expect more developments regarding the ICMS removal on imported diesel (or at least another solution that could mitigate or dilute the effect of the R$1.17/liter ICMS tax charge on diesel), which could be a relief in terms of domestic supply as importers could be once again enticed to import.

Fuel Distribution - All eyes on volumes/market share, while investors continue gauging profitability for March/April (and 1Q26). We keep an eye on (i) the fuel distribution volume/market share data, which usually comes out around the 20th day of the month, as this could show how incumbents have been capturing incremental volumes YTD and (ii) continued updates from the fuel market, as we believe some distributors have been running with EBITDA margins of R$250-300/m³. This could provide a meaningful tailwind for distributors, in our view, allowing them to comfortably finance the step-up in working capital expected from higher-priced fuel. We expect investors to continue assess the potential upside from higher margins in March/April, while we note that 1Q26 margins could sustain above R$200/m³ as flagged in our recent note <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118774> .

Braskem will report its 4Q25 earnings this week and we have been receiving many questions from investors regarding the investment case, especially given (i) higher resins prices, (ii) higher freight costs, (iii) the presidential sanction on the bump in REIQ for 2026, and (iv) the imminent change in shareholders' structure. While the ongoing conflict in the Middle East could accelerate capacity shutdowns globally, we believe it remains early to see a structural shift in the sentiment towards the petrochemical cycle. We believe Braskem's short-term earnings outlook could improve on the back of higher resins prices (and higher freight into Brazil) and the delay in COGS and expect management to provide more details on this dynamics on Friday during the 4Q25 earnings call.

Our Thoughts on the Crude Oil Market

We expect oil prices to remain supported on the back of: (i) persistent and escalating disruptions to Middle East flows, with tanker traffic through the Strait of Hormuz still constrained (despite some movement from tankers by some countries); (ii) material supply losses across Gulf producers, as exports remain curtailed and some production has been shut in or rerouted due to logistical bottlenecks and infrastructure damage; and (iii) a sharp repricing of non-Middle East barrels, with global buyers bidding up alternative grades as refiners urge to replace missing medium-sour supply. Most recently, the tensions have risen as the U.S. threatened to attack Iranian nuclear power infrastructure and Iran responded saying they could further attack energy infrastructure and water desalination across Gulf countries - we believe this could add further geopolitical risk premium into Brent.

The Trader's Corner: Insights from the Desk

Multiples remained broadly stable over the week, with PRIO standing out as short interest and days to cover declined sharply. Brava also saw a continued reduction in both metrics, extending last week’s momentum. On the earnings side, Petrobras and PRIO recorded upward EBITDA revisions, reinforcing their better position amid the Upstream players to capture Brent upside.
