Semma reiterates constructive view on mall sector


We hosted a meeting with Semma (consultancy specialized in shopping mall planning, development, and operations), represented by founding partner Marcos Semenzin. Main takeaways: (i) dominant assets continue to outperform the broader industry; (ii) tenant mix has been shifting toward higher-quality retailers; (iii) the Tax Reform could represent friction for the sector; (iv) investments in greenfields remain off the table (for now); and (v) anchor stores are expanding their footprint.


Prime assets and high-quality tenants have outperformed


Mr. Semenzin noted that the mall sector is delivering solid metrics, with most assets maintaining vacancy rates below 5% (a level he defines as “controlled vacancy”). He highlighted a clear trend of mature, AAA assets outperforming the broader sector. In a way, this view reinforces our thesis that listed malls have been industry leaders, which is further supported by the performance gap between these players and the industry average (with consistently higher SSS, lower vacancy rates, etc.). He added that malls have been making efforts to diversify their tenant mix and skew it toward higher-quality retailers.


Tax Reform should trigger negotiations between tenants and malls


Another key talking point was the ongoing Tax Reform and its impact on malls. While the incorporation of VAT into rents would be positive for malls’ top line, Mr. Semenzin is skeptical about a full pass-through without extensive negotiation with tenants. He noted that if tenants were to absorb the full tax burden, occupancy costs would exceed 15%-20% of sales (a level he identifies as the upper limit for sustainable operations). If tenants attempt to pass these costs on to the end consumer, the resulting price increases would likely weigh on demand. He believes the Tax Reform should create friction in tenant-landlord relations, with negotiations taking place to protect tenants’ financial health.


Brownfields outrank greenfields in terms of sector investment priorities


Regarding the investment outlook for the sector, Mr. Semenzin reinforced a cautious stance on new developments, noting limited visibility for greenfield projects, as the expected IRR remains below the cost of equity in the current high interest rate environment (he does think some smaller cities have pent-up demand for malls). In this context, expansions have become increasingly attractive for mall operators, offering a more compelling risk-adjusted return. This shift toward expansions is further supported by robust demand from anchor tenants seeking to expand their footprint, alongside an emerging trend of international retailers entering dominant Brazilian malls.
