BZ Insurance Sector: January SUSEP data point to a solid start to the year for BBSE, CXSE and Porto

 

Hi,

 

The Brazilian insurance regulator, SUSEP, released sector data for January, providing an initial read on how 1Q26 is shaping up for the main listed players.

 

Based on SUSEP’s data, we estimate a net income of R$774mn for BB Seguridade (BBSE) in January (-9% m/m and +8% y/y). While it is still early to draw firm conclusions from a single month, annualizing the figure (3x) points to a net income of ~R$2.3bn for 1Q26 (flat q/q; +16% y/y), around 8% above consensus.

 

For Caixa Seguridade (CXSE), SUSEP data suggest net income of ~R$393mn in January (+5% m/m and +19% y/y). Annualizing the monthly result implies ~R$1.18bn for 1Q26 (+5% q/q and +17% y/y), ~3% above both our estimate and consensus.

 

For Porto, SUSEP disclosed net income of R$152mn in January (+19% m/m and +48% y/y), with a positive contribution from lower loss ratios. Assuming SUSEP figures represent ~70% of consolidated earnings (in line with recent quarters), this would imply a net income of ~R$1.25bn for the quarter (+49% q/q and +50% y/y), significantly above both our estimates and consensus, although still based on a single data point.

 

Below, we highlight the main trends for each company:

 

>>BB Seguridade

 

January SUSEP data suggest a solid start to the quarter, with earnings supported mainly by BrasilPrev.

 

BrasilSeg

*	Net income reached R$383mn (-20% m/m; +3% y/y).
*	Written premiums increased 10% m/m (-5% y/y), while earned premiums declined 6% m/m (-15% y/y).
*	Loss ratio increased 6.9p.p m/m (but improved 12.5p.p y/y).
*	Acquisition costs fell 4% m/m (+2% y/y), with the ratio rising to 33% (+0.9p.p m/m; +5.4p.p y/y).
*	SG&A declined 41% m/m (+5% y/y), with G&A ratio at 9% (-3.5p.p m/m; +1.6p.p y/y).
*	Financial result came in at R$104mn (-19% m/m; flat y/y).

 

BrasilPrev

*	Net income reached R$194mn (+7% m/m; +79% y/y).
*	Contributions nearly doubled m/m to R$6.3bn (+23% y/y).
*	Acquisition costs were flat m/m (-2% y/y), while SG&A declined 49% m/m (-19% y/y).
*	Financial results improved meaningfully to R$113mn (vs. R$34mn in Dec and a loss in Jan/25).

 

BrasilCap

*	Net income reached R$30mn (-70% m/m; +195% y/y).
*	Revenues increased 9% m/m (-5% y/y) to R$455mn.
*	Acquisition costs rose 8% m/m (flat y/y), while SG&A declined 46% m/m (+5% y/y).
*	Financial results came in at R$30mn (-50% m/m; +561% y/y).

 

BB Broker 

*	As per our estimates, BB Broker’s revenues was R$493mn in January (+3% m/m and -9% y/y).

 

>>Caixa Seguridade

 

January SUSEP data suggest a solid start to the quarter, with stronger contributions from insurance subsidiaries and continued growth in pension.

*	The sequential increase in net income was mainly driven by stronger results from XS3 (mortgage run-on: +13% m/m; +37% y/y) and Too Seguros (+74% m/m; -3% y/y), both benefiting from stronger premium growth and lower expenses.
*	Gross pension contributions increased 9% m/m to R$2.5bn (+12% y/y), while net inflows rose 25% m/m to R$651mn (+573% y/y).
*	Insurance premiums were flat m/m at R$770mn (+4% y/y), with weaker performance in credit life (-11% m/m; -38% y/y) offset by stronger mortgage volumes (+4% m/m; +13% y/y).
*	Loss ratio declined 4.8p.p y/y to 22.7% (+0.4p.p m/m), with improvements across segments, especially mortgage.
*	Capitalization contributions declined 2% m/m to R$183mn (+23% y/y).

 

Porto Seguro

 

January SUSEP data indicate a strong start to the quarter, mainly driven by improved loss ratio.

*	Net income reached R$152mn (+19% m/m; +48% y/y).
*	Written premiums totaled R$1.9bn (-14% m/m; +5% y/y), while earned premiums were flat m/m (+12% y/y).
*	Claims expenses declined 3% m/m (+5% y/y), leading to a loss ratio of 49.7% (-3.2p.p m/m; -1.4p.p y/y).
*	Acquisition costs declined 5% m/m (+23% y/y), with commission ratio at ~22% (-1.2p.p m/m; +2.1p.p y/y).
*	SG&A declined 27% m/m (+1% y/y), with G&A ratio at 14% (-4.2p.p m/m; -1.1p.p y/y).
*	Financial results increased to R$66mn (+83% m/m; +24% y/y).

 

While it is still difficult to draw firm conclusions from a single month of data, assuming SUSEP figures account for ~70% of Porto’s reported bottom line (the same proportion seen in the last quarter), we estimate net income of R$1.25bn for 1Q26 (+49% q/q; +50% y/y), which is 62% above our estimate and 43% above consensus.
