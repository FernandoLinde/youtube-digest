Brazil

Fiscal Update – February Preview: the primary deficit is expected to reach R$30.7bn, broadly in line with our original forecast (R$31.7bn) and better than market consensus (R$34.3bn). Net revenue growth remains robust, accelerating to 3.3% y/y YTD in real terms.  Real primary spending rose 3.8% y/y YTD (vs. 2.6% through January), driven by stronger discretionary spending. 1st bimonthly report: higher oil prices should support revenue, leading us to expect no spending freeze and a spending block slightly below R$5bn, amid weaker pension and BPC dynamics. Geopolitical Shock: Rule of thumb: even with oil at $65, the export tax adds R$26bn in annual revenue; each additional US$10 implies a further R$20bn for the federal government. Measures presented so far:  Temporary elimination of federal taxes on diesel through end-May costs R$4.7bn; if extended through year-end, the cost rises to R$18bn. Diesel subsidy capped at R$10bn or through year-end, whichever comes first. Excluded from the spending limit. The new export tax on oil (12%) and diesel (50%) is projected to generate ~R$3bn per month, with no formal expiration date. 2026 primary deficit revised down to R$45bn (from R$50bn), but uncertainty remains high amid oil-related revenue risks and potential policy responses. Our base case incorporates a transitory oil price shock, with no additional policy response. (Full story here) <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118892> 

With the IPCA-15 (Mar) print due out this Thursday, and with the Copom minutes due out tomorrow, the Focus (Weekly Market Readout) for the week ending March 20 featured another upward revision to the Selic benchmark rate at YE26, from 12.25% to 12.50% p.a. Now, the market expects 50bps cuts in the April, June, August and September meetings, and a final 25bps cut in the November meeting. No more cuts are expected in the December meeting. For its part, YE26 IPCA inflation consensus was revised upwards once again, from 4.10% to 4.17% y/y. (Full story here) <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118905> 

In the third week of March (Mar 16–20), the trade balance posted a US$1.4bn surplus, with US$7.1bn in exports more than offsetting US$5.7bn in imports. In March (up to the 20th), the trade surplus totaled US$5.2bn, with the daily average of exports and imports posting declines of 4.0% y/y and 0.1% y/y, respectively. (Full story here) <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118899>

Colombia

Colombia faces significant fiscal and liquidity pressures in 2026, increasing risks around its sovereign debt profile. Financing needs remain elevated, with the government relying heavily on short-term issuance, distorting the yield curve and pushing borrowing costs higher. Gross debt remains close to 65% of GDP, with no clear signs of fiscal adjustment, undermining investor confidence and raising the risk premium. The combination of high dependence on short-term funding, government interventions, and the absence of a credible fiscal strategy points to persistent pressure on rates and heightened volatility. This underscores the urgency of implementing a credible fiscal consolidation plan to prevent further increases in financing costs and risks. (Full story here) <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118885>
