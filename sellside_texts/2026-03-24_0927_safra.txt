Last week in summary. Most global markets had another negative week. In the United States, the Fed's decision to maintain interest rates was accompanied by a more cautious tone due to the rise in oil prices, which could put pressure on inflation; concerns were added to higher-than-expected producer inflation data. In Europe, the maintenance of interest rates was also announced with concerns about energy prices, in addition to higher trade deficit data. In China, indices also fell, despite better-than-expected activity data. In Brazil, Bacen began cutting interest rates at a more timid pace, and the IBC-Br came in slightly below expectations. The USD remained practically stable vs. the BRL (closing at BRL 5.31/USD), and the interest rate curve showed a strong increase in the medium term (2027–2029). The 5-year CDS closed the week at 150 points (vs. 138 points in the previous week). Even so, foreign investors injected USD 5.2 billion into the Brazilian stock market until March 18.

The week ahead: What to expect? For this week in the US, the week begins with Chicago activity index data on Monday (03/23), PMI on Tuesday (03/24), and consumer confidence data on Friday (03/27). In Europe, several countries release PMIs on Tuesday and Germany releases economic sentiment data on Wednesday (25/03). In Brazil, the market awaits the Copom minutes on Tuesday, the IPCA-15, the INCC and monetary policy report on Thursday (26/03), and the April tariff flag in addition to the PNAD on Friday.
