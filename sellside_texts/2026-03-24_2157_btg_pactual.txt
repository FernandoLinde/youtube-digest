Better-than-expected Q4 results

Adj. EBITDA margin reached 22.6%

Mater Dei delivered better-than-expected Q4 results, supported by continued operating leverage gains and efficiency improvements. Net revenue grew 15% y/y to R$562mn (3% above BTGe), while EBITDA increased 64% y/y to R$127mn, beating our estimate by 5%. As a result, EBITDA margin expanded 670bps y/y and 40bps q/q, reaching 22.6% (+50bps vs. BTGe). After lower-than-expected net financial expenses, net income came in at R$37mn, up 51% y/y and 27% above our estimate.

Mix-driven growth and strong cost dilution

Net revenue growth was mainly driven by a higher average ticket (+9% y/y), reflecting a better mix of procedures, increased complexity (notably in oncology), and annual price adjustments, while volumes remained softer sequentially given seasonality (operating capacity at 1,207 beds, -39 q/q). Occupancy increased 3.2p.p. y/y to 74.9%, reinforcing the company’s strategy of improving asset utilization. Cost discipline remained a key highlight, with costs declining 4.0p.p. as a percentage of revenue y/y, driven by better dilution of fixed costs, particularly in personnel and medical services. SG&A also showed positive trends, declining 3.1p.p. y/y (as % of revenue), reflecting ongoing efficiency gains and a leaner corporate structure.

Cash generation remains solid; leverage under control

FCF to firm (after capex) reached R$73mn (vs. R$20mn in 4Q24), supported by strong EBITDA expansion and lower capex in the quarter (R$20mn vs. R$26mn in 4Q24). Net debt (ex-M&A payables and leasing) increased modestly q/q (+R$63mn) to R$800mn, primarily reflecting shareholder remuneration, with buybacks and dividends totaling ~R$454mn in the period. Even so, leverage improved to 1.7x ND/EBITDA (vs. 1.8x in 3Q), reinforcing the company’s comfortable financial position and ongoing deleveraging trend.

Recovery cycle gaining traction; Buy reiterated

Q4 results reinforce our view that Mater Dei is in a sustained recovery cycle, with improving margins and operating efficiency translating into stronger earnings. Despite typical seasonality, performance remained solid, suggesting that recent initiatives (ramp-up of acquired assets, better mix, and tighter cost control) are structurally lifting profitability. We expect margins to remain at or above current levels. The stock still trades at an attractive valuation (~10x 2026 earnings), which we believe does not fully reflect the recovery in fundamentals and cash generation. We reiterate Mater Dei as our favorite small-cap in healthcare, combining solid earnings momentum with an attractive entry point.
