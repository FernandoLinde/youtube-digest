Aguas Andinas: A Clear Quarter With Upside Ahead

Aguas Andinas reported an in-line 4Q performance, with revenues reaching CLP192bn, up 7.0% y/y, mainly driven by tariff indexation (+8.8% y/y) as billed volumes increased only 0.6% y/y. Operating costs rose 6.5% y/y, growing below the top line. As a result, Ebitda increased 7.4% y/y to CLP97bn, with the Ebitda margin expanding marginally by 21bps to 50.5%. Net income reached CLP43bn, representing a solid 37% y/y increase, driven by the stronger operating performance and lower readjustment units as inflationary pressures eased compared to the same period last year. All figures were in line with our estimates. The indebtedness ratio remained broadly stable at 1.39x, while ND/Ebitda declined from 3.74x in 4Q24 to 3.60x in 4Q25. Overall, this was a solid quarter, with high single-digit Ebitda growth, although we continue to see room for margin expansion, particularly as tariffs were set to increase above local inflation. We believe operating leverage has yet to fully materialize, as the tariff reset is only partially in effect and will continue to be rolled out during the current year. We expect margins to improve gradually in 2026, with a more meaningful expansion by 2027. At the same time, provisions for bad debt remain at healthy levels, while maintenance costs appear to be normalizing, both of which should help ease margin pressure in the coming quarters. Electricity costs also declined during the period and should continue trending lower, as new contracts come into effect at more favorable rates, alongside ongoing efforts to optimize consumption by shifting from regulated to unregulated supply. Overall, we expect the full impact of the tariff reset and operating leverage to materialize over the next two years. We remain buyers. Peruvian Utilities: Gas crisis comes to an end, but 1Q26 damages remain

Transportadora de Gas del Perú (TGP) announced over the weekend the completion of repair works on its natural gas pipeline following a leak that occurred earlier in the month in Cusco, enabling the progressive restoration of gas transport and liquids. The restoration process involved complex technical operations to equalize pressure across the 730-kilometer pipeline, with a gradual normalization of service prioritizing residential users, healthcare facilities, and public transportation before expanding to large industries and power generators. After two challenging weeks, this puts an end to the energy emergency and supply restrictions that had impacted various sectors of the Peruvian economy. Spot prices in the Santa Rosa node have already normalized to ~US$30/MWh, as has gas generation. That said, we estimate that Colbun’s Fenix Power will report a negative Ebitda in the quarter.

 

Weather: IRI says chances of El Niño in May-July at 78%

The International Research Institute (IRI) at Columbia published its latest ENSO forecast in which they see big chances of El Niño developing from May onwards, which differs quite a lot from what NOAA published last week. The official forecasts from NOAA and the IRI are often quite similar. However, occasionally they may differ noticeably. There can be several possible reasons for differences. One is the human forecasters, using their experience and judgment, may disagree to some degree with the models, which may have known biases. Another reason is the models are not run at the same time that the forecasters make their assessment, so that the starting ENSO conditions may be slightly different between the two times. The charts are updated at two different times of the month, typically about a week apart, with the IRI forecast run later. IRI sees a 72% chance of El Niño in the May-July period (NOAA below 50%) and an 80%probability in the June-August window (vs just north of 60% from NOAA).
