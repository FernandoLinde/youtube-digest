IQVIA data for Feb’26, published by Sindusfarma (Pharmaceutical Industry Association), point to solid LTM industry trends despite a monthly slowdown. In nominal terms, February sell‑out rose 7.5% y/y, versus 10.7% in January and 19.4% in December. In our view, while we may have some calendar impacts from Carnival, this deceleration likely suggests an undersupply of GLP‑1 drugs in early ’26. The slowdown is largely driven by reference products growth that decelerated to 5% in January and 2% in February versus +15% in 2H25. We also would have expected some acceleration at this time of year ahead of annual drug price increases, which was not the case. Sell‑in increased 2.8% y/y, down from 5% in January. Volume growth was 1.3% y/y, from 1.9%. Still, on an LTM basis metrics remained strong, with sell‑out at 12%, sell‑in at 7.4%, and volume growth at 3.8%. 

*	Retail channel: Drugstore sell-out decelerated, likely reflecting GLP-1 undersupply. Sell-out for drug retail in Brazil slowed to 10.9% y/y, from 11.7% in January and 15.4 in December. On an LTM basis, drug retail sell-out growth remains solid at 12.8% y/y—implying continued positive top-line trends for RD Saúde (RADL; OW), which, in our view, is likely to outperform the market again in ’26 given its higher exposure to GLP-1 and recent channel-optimization initiatives. It is also important to flag that potential GLP-1 undersupply could have had a limited impact of RADL, as the company invested heavily in working capital in 4Q25 and likely holds higher category inventory vs peers. For Hypera (HYPE; OW), medium-term top-line trends are expected to remain softer than the market given its limited and delayed participation in the weight-loss segment. 
*	Institutional channel: Sell-in now decelerating m/m. Sell-in to the institutional channel contracted 10.4% y/y in February, vs. January -9%. On a LTM basis, institutional sell-in growth was 0.8% y/y as of February, likely reflecting limited purchases in the public segment.
