R$3.3bn estimated capex

On March 27th, ANEEL will hold the first transmission auction of the year. The auction will feature 5 blocks with a total estimated capex of R$3.4bn, 274 km of transmission lines, 825 MVA of substations, five synchronous compensators, and a maximum RAP of R$580mn (17.3% RAP/Capex). The projects are spread across 11 states - RJ, SP, MG, PR, SC, RN, CE, BA, SE, MT and PA - with energization deadlines of up to 60 months. Block 3 is the largest (capex of R$1.4bn; RAP of R$232mn), followed by Block 5 (capex of R$1.0bn; RAP of R$186mn), which together account for 71% of total capex.

Blocks initially awarded to MEZ not included

The auction was initially expected to include five additional blocks originally awarded to MEZ Energia in the 2020 and 2021 auctions but not delivered. However, under the agreement between MEZ Energia and the MME, one of these blocks (Block 6) will remain with MEZ, with an extended deadline for delivery, while the other four blocks (7, 8, 9, and 10) were postponed to a second auction session, with the date still to be defined.

Auction details

Block 3 is divided into four sublots and bidders are required to submit five distinct proposals: one for the entire block and one for each sublot (which could be zero for one of the sublots). In case valid bids are received for both the full block and the individual sublots, the winning option will be the one with the lowest combined RAP. If any sublot receives no bid, the remaining sublots will not be auctioned.

Another competitive auction

Given that this is the first auction of the year and that the blocks are relatively small, we expect a competitive auction. Among our coverage, Taesa, Engie, Axia and CPFL may participate.

R$886mn NPV

The implied real regulatory WACC was set at 9.11%, with a real cost of equity of 12.50%. Based on the auction framework, we estimate a total NPV of R$886mn, discounting the cap-bid FCFE at a real 8.5% Ke and assuming 70% capex leverage at IPCA+7%. For Blocks 3, 4 and 5, we assumed a 100% SUDAM/SUDENE tax benefit. However, bidders could outperform our estimates through capex, leverage, and construction efficiencies.
