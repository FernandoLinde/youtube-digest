Continued Inflows Across the Andean Region

In February, Chilean institutional investors showed positive flows. With US$175.7 mn of inflows into Chilean stocks, pension funds extended the positive trend in place since August last year. Compared with the prior month, inflows increased; however, the allocation to local equities declined from 10.8% to 10.3% of total AUM.Latam Airlines emerged as the most purchased stock by pension funds this month, resulting in inflows of US$161.4 mn, followed by Falabella with US$18.0 mn and Banco de Chile with US$16.4 mn. On the divestment side, the largest sales were in Enel Americas (-US$24.0mn), Entel (-US$13.4 mn), and Enel Chile (-US$10.1 mn). Similarly, Chilean mutual funds experienced inflows of US$59.4 mn, while decreasing their exposure to local equities to 4.8% during February. These inflows were primarily driven by Enel Americas, the most purchased stock at US$54.0 mn, followed by Latam Airlines at US$38.1mn and Itau Chile at US$25.6 mn. Conversely, they were mostly net sellers of ILC (-US$48.1 mn), followed by MallPlaza (-US$36.6 mn) and CCU (-US$16.1 mn). In Colombia, pension funds were net buyers, with US$23.2mn of inflows in the second month of 2026. Meanwhile, the Colombian market had an ADTV of US$64.1 mn, almost double its 10Y historical average. Finally, Peruvian pension funds posted an AUM of US$35.2bn, from which 18.1% are local equities. 

*	Chilean pension funds began the year with net inflows, recording a positive net inflow of US$175.65mn in February. This figure continues the trend observed last month. The allocation to local equities decreased versus last month but remains elevated relative to prior years. The largest holdings in pension fund portfolios are LATAM Airlines (US$3.6bn), SQM‑B (US$2.8bn), and MallPlaza (US$2.5bn). 
*	Chilean mutual funds registered US$59.4mn of inflows into local equities in February, down from January’s US$360.6mn, but still consistent with a firm inflow trend this year. The allocation to equities edged to 4.8%, and year to date the pattern continues to point to renewed appetite for domestic stocks and solid institutional conviction. Buying was led by Enel Américas (US$54.0mn), with LATAM Airlines (US$38.1mn) and Itaú Chile (US$25.6mn) close behind. 
*	Colombian pension funds remain buyers. Pension funds registered net inflows of US$23.3 million in February, while their exposure to local equities decreased to 8.8% for the month. The Colombian market’s ADTV was US$64.1mn, nearly twice its 10‑year historical average.
