Cemig (CMIG4.SA): 4Q25 First Take: Better-than-expected 
	
20 March 2026 | 10:38AM BRT | Research | Equity 	
	
	
On March 19th, after market closing, Cemig announced 4Q25 results with an adjusted EBITDA (ex-VNR, one-offs and equity income) of BRL 1.86bn (-3% YoY), a beat to both GSe (BRL 1.72bn) and Bloomberg consensus (BRL 1.67bn). Bottom line followed reaching BRL 1.9bn (vs consensus' BRL 1.1bn and our BRL 789mn), also positively impacted by post-employment obligations agreements amounting to BRL 788mn. Key highlights from the set of results follow:

●

Operational results. Distribution adjusted EBITDA (ex-VNR) reached BRL 958mn (+4% YoY), a beat to our BRL 834mn. Billed volumes' growth of negative 4% was offset by a B Parcel positive effect of BRL 138mn in the quarter. Generation and transmission (including trading) adjusted EBITDA reached BRL 660mn (-31% YoY), a miss to our BRL 750mn dragged by a negative result in the generation arm driven by lower GSF and worse exposure to higher power prices in the quarter. Additionally, Gas operations were mostly in line with our numbers while holding and others costs amounted to positive BRL 56mn (vs our negative BRL 53mn forecast). Overall, results were better than expected driven by solid results in the distribution arm. 

●

Leverage continued increasing reaching ~2.3x ND/EBITDA (vs our 2.2x forecast), which compares to ~1.8x last quarter and 1.3x 4Q24, while total investments in 2025 reached BRL 6.6bn, mostly in line with planned capex of BRL 6.4bn for the year.

We remain Sell-rated on Cemig. While we acknowledge that management has been implementing cost efficiency measures and selling non-core assets, to the extent that Cemig remains an SOE we see limited room for further relevant cost efficiency gains, we see the company trading at 6.8% real IRR in a context of likely declining DY.
