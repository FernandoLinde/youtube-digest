Mexico Consumer Update – March

Quick take: ANTAD data remains weak with February SSS data for supermarkets at 1.1% (YTD now at 2.0%). Any degree of new year-new me wishful thinking with regard to easier comps or an improvement in purchasing power is being quickly eroded with continued soft data points from the Mexican consumer. On the macro front, consumer loans increased 12% YoY with NPL levels under control and employment dynamics remain sluggish albeit growing (1.2% YoY as per IMSS data). On the other hand, remittances declined 1.4% (in USD); imported capital goods decreased by 1% YoY, and F&B inflation accelerated to 5.6% YoY. Over the past month, we updated Walmex <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118543>  (still Neutral, still stuck; provide Walmex Day preview) and Alsea <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118259>  (raised #’s and target on better margin prospects; liked new messaging at investor day). We also got a hefty strategic update <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118778>  from FEMSA alongside 4Q results. Our top pick remains Tiendas 3B. To a lesser degree, we are upbeat on Alsea, Bimbo and KOF.

 

Our take on ANTAD February retail sales data: weak

ANTAD reported SSS data for the month of February: Total SSS +1.1% YoY. By format: Supermarkets +1.1% (following January at 2.9%), Dept Stores -0.7%, Specialized +3.4%. By category: Grocery +2.4%, Apparel/Footwear -2.1%, General Merchandise +1.0%. Our sense is that YTD data (SSS for food retailers at 2.0%, dept stores in negative territory) is a bit worse than what were low expectations but that included some degree of wishful thinking with regard to easy comps and/or an improvement in consumer activity. The skinny is that we aren’t returning to MSD growth range with easier comps and that the Mexican consumer remains under pressure. On the supermarket front, we’ve heard of limited changes in promotional activity relative to last year.

  

Our take on the latest macro data points

On the positive front, consumer loans increased +12% YoY in Jan with healthy NPL levels at 3.4%, resilient formal employment (IMSS) dynamics in Feb (+1.2%); consumer confidence increased sequentially in Feb to 44.5 (from 44.3 in Jan); On the negative front, remittances declined 1% YoY in USD in Jan; imported capital goods (one of our favorite proxy for FDI) declined 1% YoY in Jan; auto sales grew 0.5% YoY and declined 10.2% sequentially in Feb; and Feb F&B inflation accelerated sequentially to 5.6% (from 4.4% in Jan).
