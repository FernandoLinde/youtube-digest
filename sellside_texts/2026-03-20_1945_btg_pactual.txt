Slower top line; stronger recurring margins 


Read the full document here >> <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118846>  


Healthy recurring net profit; dividend payout increased to 50%


Wiz reported 4Q25 results, with GAAP net income coming in 14% above our estimate at R$49mn (-10.5% q/q; +29% y/y). While the headline print came below our expectations, it is important to highlight a couple of points: (i) there was a ~R$10mn impairment impact with no cash effect; (ii) the sale of Wiz Concept at the end of October impacted the bottom line by ~R$1.8mn, which we had not accounted for in our estimates. Adjusting for these effects, the bottom line was actually ~7% above our estimate at ~R$60mn. Despite slower top-line growth, margins benefited mainly from lower financial expenses. Wiz generated almost R$220mn in cash flow in 2025 (15% FCF), and with net debt/EBITDA now below 0.5x and debt duration extended, the company decided to increase the payout ratio from 25% to 50%, in line with our model.


Weaker top-line growth; R$10mn impairment impacted GAAP EPS


Net revenue declined 5% q/q (+6% y/y; 5% below our estimate), impacted by the sale of Wiz Concept and lower revenues from BMG Broker, BRB Broker, and Promotiva. On the other hand, we saw strong performance from Wiz Corporate, where revenues increased 32% q/q and 16% y/y. We also highlight the improvement in equity income (+18% q/q; +12% y/y), mainly driven by results from Inter Seguros. Costs and expenses rose 12% q/q (+11% y/y; 13% above our estimate), mainly impacted by a one-off impairment of R$10mn, largely related to Wiz Corporate, whose goodwill had not been revised for nearly 12 years. The company also made progress in improving efficiency, with headcount (ex-Wiz Concept) down 4% q/q to 746. Finally, financial expenses declined ~52% q/q to R$7mn (-72% y/y).


Stock down 10% since our downgrade; bias improved, but still Neutral


Last month, we decided to downgrade Wiz following its Investor Day (link <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/117769> ). The rationale was straightforward: the stock had risen nearly 80% over the previous 12 months, and although we remained constructive on the story, the combination of a more demanding valuation and doubts around BRB Seguros’ growth going forward (~25% of EBITDA) led us to take profits. Since then, the stock has declined 10%, mainly pressured by the global sell-off observed amid the conflict in the Middle East. That said, trading at 6.1x 2026 P/E, our bias has improved a bit, but we remain Neutral, mainly until we have greater clarity on what to expect from BRB Seguros going forward.
