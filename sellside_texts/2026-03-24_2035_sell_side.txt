Gregory Shearer is an Executive Director in Global Commodities Research based in London. As Head of Base and Precious Metals Strategy at J.P. Morgan, he specializes in the fundamental analysis of supply and demand to develop price outlooks across metals markets. Originally from the United States, Gregory graduated from Miami University, earning Bachelor's degrees in Finance and Entrepreneurship.

 
Patrick Jones covers the European Mining, Steel, & Hydrogen sectors. He joined J.P. Morgan in September of 2018 from Deutsche Bank, where he covered the European metals & mining space for two years. Prior to Deutsche Bank, he spent six years at Nomura also covering the metals & mining sector, including commodities. His research focuses include the Copper & Aluminium subsectors as well as Sustainable Investing & Climate change issues for the Mining & Steel sectors. He initiated coverage on the EMEA Hydrogen sector in early 2021. He also co-leads the J.P. Morgan Global Hydrogen Week and has presented to several corporate management teams and industry conferences on the topics of hydrogen & climate change. Patrick twice finished in the top ten among all UK analysts in the Starmine/Refinitive analyst awards for recommendation performance. Patrick graduated with a Bachelor’s of Science in Philosophy & Economics from the London School of Economics & Political Science.

 
Inga Q Galeni, a Vice President, joined J.P. Morgan in 2019 and works in the CEEMEA & South Africa Equity Strategy team. The team was ranked #1 in Institutional Investor’s survey for EMEA Equity Strategy in 2025 and #2 for EMEA South Africa Research in 2024. Prior to joining J.P. Morgan, Inga worked in Rand Merchant Bank as a Credit Portfolio Analyst. Inga graduated with a Bachelor of Commerce in Finance and holds a post graduate Honours degree in Investment Management from the University of Johannesburg. He holds an Msc in Economic Policy from SOAS University of London.
