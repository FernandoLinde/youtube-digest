Strong revenue and decent FCF drove a solid Q4

EPS of R$1.55 (+41% y/y; exactly what we had); ROE of 27%


Cyrela's 4Q25 results were solid: (i) nice P&L expansion y/y (in line with us), due to strong revenues (more on that below) and (ii) decent FCF generation (slightly above our call), on big dividends from subsidiaries. Net revenues totaled R$3.23bn (+29% y/y, in line with us). Adjusted gross margin was 33.7% (+40bps y/y; 30bps below us), impacted by the sale of compact units in Vista Milano project, and EPS was R$1.55 (+41% y/y; exactly what we had), leading to a strong annualized ROE of 27%.


Our two cents on Cyrela's big revenues in Q4


As aforementioned, Q4 revenues were boosted by a change in accounting rules. Prior to Q4, Cyrela would wait (i) either 6 months after launch or (ii) until the project reaches 50% of sales, before starting to book revenues. But, since the company has never quit a project after its launch, Cyrela is now booking revenues once its sales team decides that it will go ahead with the project. Consequently, we had big revenue recognition from some projects in Q4 (e.g., Epic and Vista Milano, with R$328mn and R$131mn, respectively, plus R$302mn came from projects launched in 4Q25).


FCF generation of R$74mn; ND/Equity of 17%


Cyrela posted a decent R$74mn FCF generation in 4Q25 (slightly above our R$50mn forecast). Cashflow generation was also helped by the collection of ~R$240mn from its JVs (mostly Cury and Lavvi), which opted to bring forward dividends to Q4 to avoid taxation. Cyrela also announced R$1.4bn in dividends in Q4, which drove its net debt to R$1.97bn, resulting in a 17% ND/Equity ratio, which is above historical average, but still pretty comfortable (mostly in light of the strong FCF we expect in 2026).


Solid (in line) 4Q25, with FCF a tad above us; Buy rated (6x P/E '26E)


Q4 was solid: (i) EPS was in line with us, with strong top line growth and 27% ROE; and (ii) FCF generation was slightly above us, helped by its JVs. We expect a positive market reaction tomorrow, but we highlight that most of the P&L expansion was driven by an accounting adjustment in the company’s revenue recognition. Despite a more challenging environment for the mid/high-income segment (recently, we discussed that in two reports: 2026 Outlook: Look before you leap <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/109585>  and SP's mid/high-end inventory under the microscope <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/117091> ), we keep our Buy rating on Cyrela, our Top Pick in the mid/high-income segment at 6x P/E 2026E.
