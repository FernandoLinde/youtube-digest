Galaxy Brain: Did anyone see NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang's unveil of the Vera Rubin Space-1 Module <https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/space-computing> ? Our investor discussions have been increasingly moving towards atmospheric and exo-atmospheric compute architectures. As more robots become connected in a 'Distributed Inference Cloud' and more objects get launched into space in a 'Distributed Orbital Cloud' we have the emergence of a hybrid compute model - a swarming intelligence of interconnected 'nodes' comprising one artificial brain. The metaphor of human brain - nearly 100bn neurons running at 20 watts (the same number of stars in the Milky Way galaxy) - may come to mind. For evidence of what a swarm of inference computers can do when working together in competition or as a team? Take a look at the world around you.

250 Miles: Space is far away. Or is it? The International Space Station (ISS) orbits the Earth at an average altitude of 250 miles above the Earth’s surface. The average low Earth orbit (LEO) communications satellite orbits the Earth at around 300 to 340 miles. To help visualize the distance of the ISS, imagine the Earth as the size of an NBA basketball (a diameter of 9.45 inches at 8.5 PSI). 250 miles would be roughly the width of a standard pencil eraser… or the width of the ‘meat’ of your earlobe. I’m currently writing this note on a train from Zurich to Geneva. If I were to use an AI chatbot on this train, I may very well be routed to a datacenter in Memphis - an air distance of approximately 7,800 kilometers (approx 4,850 miles). That’s a long way to travel to use Tennessee’s electricity and water for a 15 second dancing cat video.

Atoms Matter: Interesting how scientific advancement in areas of GenAI and space exploration have reinvigorated investor interest in atoms, molecules... substances. Moving electrons and light around the Earth and in space requires a knowledge of metals and gases. After a 1-hour meeting or a 2-hour dinner, it is not unusual for clients to sigh and ask me: "What should our kids do?" It's not my place to say... but if forced I would suggest learning the elements. Periodically. Never trust an atom (they make up everything).
