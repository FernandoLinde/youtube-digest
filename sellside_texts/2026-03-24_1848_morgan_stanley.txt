ONE REPORT YOU CAN’T MISS


Risks in Private Credit – Significant, but Not Systemic 

Rising market anxiety around private credit – as is suggested by sell-offs in BDC equities, elevated redemptions, and scrutiny of Software exposure – reflects a pricing and sentiment reset rather than the start of a systemic unwind, in our view. We find no evidence of rising aggregate leverage, as non-investment-grade corporate lending relative to GDP remains contained and broadly unchanged, with private credit growth largely substituting for bank and public-market lending. Structural features – including modest leverage in BDCs, senior and well-protected bank exposure, and redemption gates designed to prevent fire sales – should limit transmission into the broader financial system. While we would expect any genuine credit cycle to produce winners and losers, we believe systemic risk concerns remain overstated relative to the underlying data and market structure.

KEY THEMATIC RESEARCH


China's AI Path: Monetizing Surging Token Use via AI Cloud 


We see China AI cloud delivering a 72% CAGR (2024-29), with Alibaba emerging as the clear leader, in our view, given its full-stack ecosystem and positioning as the best AI infrastructure play. We reiterate BABA as our Top Pick in China Internet, as we see Street-high 45% cloud growth in F2027. We are also OW Tencent for 2C AI optionality, stay EW on Baidu, and favor data center names GDS and VNET as capex cycle beneficiaries. read more <http://t.cm.morganstanley.com/r/?id=hc4642fe,e16fe8a4,ff3ee03e&e=cDE9LTUxMjc1OTY0NA&s=SJBDYSMVXVTX7jbSlaUr_eHZJgvZb6KoTHibzM3QB-E>  


Power for AI: Flexible Power – The Next Wave of Growth in AI 


“Power for AI” has emerged as a US$1.5trn theme, and we see energy storage systems (ESS) driving the next wave as the constraint shifts from availability to flexibility, volatility management, and time-to-power. We expect a ~30% CAGR in ESS deployment into 2030, with data centers potentially doubling power demand in five years. We believe ESS becomes a system-level necessity, enabling peak-shaving, mitigating bottlenecks, and accelerating a shift toward a flexible “inventory of electricity.” read more <http://t.cm.morganstanley.com/r/?id=hc4642fe,e16fe8a4,ff3ee03f&e=cDE9LTUxMjc1OTY0NA&s=Eu274aODbzNOI6w5BRGzpSs-RQDDI3_smcHm2F7YXYM>  


Space Metals: The Critical Minerals Enabling the Space Economy 


If companies and governments aim to build at scale in space, they will require numerous specialty alloys and highly engineered metals. We believe that supplying this demand will be a challenge; hence, as launch activity accelerates, metals could emerge as a meaningful bottleneck. Further, many of the materials critical to rockets and spacecraft are already subject to tight, concentrated, or geopolitically sensitive supply chains. read more <http://t.cm.morganstanley.com/r/?id=hc4642fe,e16fe8a4,ff3ee040&e=cDE9LTUxMjc1OTY0NA&s=vFyTsCMO0wAnspbjLzHvGLeP8arkT9gzPIqkUWltfOw>  


KEY MACRO RESEARCH


Global Economics: Second Order Effects of Oil Shocks 


The Strait of Hormuz disruption is triggering second-order shocks across aluminum, petrochemicals, plastics, and fertilizers, amplifying global risk beyond oil. We believe markets still underprice the scale, with over $15bn of aluminum trade (~8%), $26bn in plastics (~9%), and critical fertilizer flows exposed, threatening upstream inputs across manufacturing and agriculture. We expect supply disruptions to outlast the conflict as recovery prioritizes energy flows, leaving Emerging Markets most exposed but extending vulnerability across global supply chains. read more <http://t.cm.morganstanley.com/r/?id=hc4642fe,e16fe8a4,ff3ee041&e=cDE9LTUxMjc1OTY0NA&s=qLSMOr4uKkh96lE8bIiVL1TjGlm33A2Nvj_pPv8Pibk>  


US Equity Strategy: Deja Vu? 


The correction in US equities was very well advanced in both time and price before the events in Iran 2 weeks ago. While we can't rule out modest downside in the near term if the rate of change in the crude oil price and the dollar’s appreciation accelerate further, we maintain our view that this correction is closer to its ending stages. The main reason is that earnings growth for the S&P 500 is +13% and accelerating today, whereas it was decelerating and entering negative territory during prior oil spikes. In addition, oil spikes that ended previous cycles were ~100% Y/Y whereas this one is currently around 40%. Finally, we also point to significant fiscal support today and clear momentum in the business cycle. read more <http://t.cm.morganstanley.com/r/?id=hc4642fe,e16fe8a4,ff3ee042&e=cDE9LTUxMjc1OTY0NA&s=mwI8bsK1vHR1k2ni4D66NvUXHM1NnqBTDwNjoNZmfLs>  


FX Strategy: Prepare for the Defense Regime 


FX aligns with a high-VIX “Carry Regime,” but a shift toward weaker growth signals an approaching “Defense Regime” where we expect DXY to stall and defensive dynamics to lead. We favor JPY outperformance, while we think NOK, SEK, and NZD offer durable shorts across regimes. Despite carry-focused consensus, our positioning data show markets underprepared, creating squeeze risk in EUR, CHF, and JPY versus USD. read more <http://t.cm.morganstanley.com/r/?id=hc4642fe,e16fe8a4,ff3ee043&e=cDE9LTUxMjc1OTY0NA&s=8s_mejlKEsmc8V9ybEU78nkIh6mtB7oFyrgRnxxMtas>  


Oil: Strait of Hormuz – Air Pocket 


The Hormuz closure is turning into a global supply loss. There are offsets that can allow some crude to bypass Hormuz altogether – Saudi Arabia’s East-West pipeline and the UAE’s Abu Dhabi Crude Oil Pipeline – but these two bypass routes could replace only a fraction of the barrels lost via Hormuz. read more <http://t.cm.morganstanley.com/r/?id=hc4642fe,e16fe8a4,ff3ee044&e=cDE9LTUxMjc1OTY0NA&s=Z-NVzRRZcZXjtV4g1JZtzKG73iquCVg7wQWg6q9USqk>

Favored OWs are Coca-Cola (Top Pick in Beverages), Philip Morris, and Mondelez (Top Pick in Food). Heidelberg Materials: Double-Upgrade to OW – Carbon Noise, Cement Pricing Power



We double-upgrade to OW with a €219 PT, as industry consolidation and value-over-volume sustain pricing and margins. Despite concerns that lower carbon prices weaken cement pricing, we see limited linkage and view Heidelberg’s recent ~30% derating as misplaced. We estimate the current share price reflects ~1.2% growth and no margin expansion, which we view as overly conservative given cost-cutting and +3–5% EBIT upside vs. consensus. read more <http://t.cm.morganstanley.com/r/?id=hc4642fe,e16fe8a4,ff3ee046&e=cDE9LTUxMjc1OTY0NA&s=xOfP4BGZkU_m4RHzBy0RLiITixszomd0Np6YCVULdRE>
