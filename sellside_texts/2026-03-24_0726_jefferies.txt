Latin America is a net exporter of crude oil. The market is focused on unwinding momentum and crowded trades, areas least affected by higher oil prices. We replaced the weak-dollar theme with hard-assets, having added fallen angels and higher oil beneficiary screens. Mexico’s retail sector showed clear signs of cooling in February, with same‑store sales (SSS) up just 1.1% YoY, the weakest monthly performance since September 2025. Total store sales rose 3.1%, also marking a deceleration versus prior months, both below inflation at 4%. February’s results highlight a notable loss of momentum, particularly compared with the rebound seen in early 2025. Over the first two months of 2026, SSS increased 2.2%, while total store sales grew 4.3%.

The release does not elaborate on the reasons for Mr Garcia's departure, however Alex Wright notes that there have been a number of management changes since Cristian Barrientos Pozo took over as CEO last year. Mr Priesca has occupied roles in finance and strategy within Walmart US, including leading financial planning and strategic initiatives supporting e-commerce and broader business performance. Quiñenco (Buy) trades at a 48% discount to its flow-through NAV of $15bn, >$7bn in assets for “free”. Brazil’s supermarket sector registered 1.95% YoY increase in food retail sales in real terms in February, according to ABRAS, marking a continuation of the gradual improvement seen since late 2025. Despite the annual gain, month‑on‑month consumption fell 3.8%, reflecting the shorter month (fewer shopping days and one fewer Saturday). The Abrasmercado basket, a proxy for essential household consumption, rose 0.47% MoM to R$ 802.88.

 

Arcos Dorados (Buy) reported a solid 4Q25, with systemwide comparable sales up 16%, supported by disciplined pricing and strong digital engagement, while total revenue grew 10.7% YoY to US$1.3bn. Adj. EBITDA rose 17.2% YoY to US$172.7m, including some tax‑related benefits, with EBITDA margin expanding 80bps (+30bs ex tax) despite persistent cost pressures in several markets. Gross‑margin recovery began to materialise in Brazil as beef inflation eased and operational efficiencies improved. A central theme during Alsea’s (Buy) Investor day was the significant white‑space opportunity, with more than 3,400 additional stores identified across existing geographies, complemented by an accelerated remodeling agenda designed to drive traffic, asset productivity, and margin expansion across the base. Starbucks, Domino’s, and full‑service restaurants target remodeling programs that lift sales between 5–15% in QSR brands and 30–50% in full‑service restaurants. Alsea reiterated confidence in long‑term sales CAGR of ~8%, driven by mid‑single‑digit SSS growth plus 2–3% organic expansion in selling area.

In contrast, air cargo division Aerosan posted only 1% YoY increase in activity as higher salmon export volumes in Chile were offset by a sharp drop in imports.
