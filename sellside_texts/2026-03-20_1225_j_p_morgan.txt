Tupy hosted its 4Q25 conference call with top management this morning. We see TUPY trading at 4.3x EV/EBITDA 26e vs POMO at 4.5x and RAPT at 4.6x. 

*	2026 outlook. For commercial vehicles, lower tariffs and higher freight prices in the US reads positively for volumes. As for off-road, investments in non-resi and data centers in the US are fuelling demand for generators and related machinery. 
*	Automotive segment. Domestic market was down 40% yoy, while foreign revenues declined 5% yoy due to the high interest rate environment and lackluster agribusiness sector. However, 41% of this segment’s top-line is linked to machining and assembly which have higher margins. 
*	Aftermarket segment. Top-line growth of 15% yoy in 4Q25 domestically was due to an increase in the portfolio of products. 
*	Energy & Decarbonization. Higher sales of own motors and indirect exports fueled revenue growth domestically of 36% yoy. 
*	Higher order activity of heavy vehicles in the US. Tupy is seeing its clients placing more orders but the clients have also cut capacity in recent quarters. As such, these higher orders should only reflect in production volumes during 2H26 as OEMs resume capacity by May. Moreover, there is limited visibility at this point on the tailwind of EPA 2027, but several contracts Tupy signed in recent quarters should start being delivered in the 2H, being a tailwind of R$600mn in top-line this year. 
*	US trucks fleet age. Fleet is at its oldest point since 2017 at ~5.8 years and is set to reach 6 years by year-end and 6.2 years by 2028. This implies substantial pent-up demand once macroeconomic risks subside and clients begin placing orders. 
*	One-offs. Totaled R$544mn in the quarter related to asset impairments, loss of tax credits and provisioning. 
*	Capacity reduction. Goal is to reduce fixed costs in order to Tupy have a positive ROIC spread to WACC in the mid-term. 
*	Operational cash flow. Benefited from R$174mn in IPI tax credits sale. 
*	Domestic outlook. Diesel price hike is a headwind for volumes recovery. 
*	Capacity utilization. Tupy is still assessing capacity and its utilization rate has not changed meaningfully vs last year (around 65% vs goal of reaching 80%). 
*	Covenant waiver. There is no need to revisit the net debt/EBITDA covenant waiver of 5.5x as the US recovery, re-structuring and new products will lead to EBITDA to improve. Moreover, Tupy’s liquidity is solid. 
*	Capex. Was in-line with the company’s expectations and linked to the demobilization initiative. 
*	Margins. Should improve sequentially and reach Tupy’s planned levels (14-15%) during 2H26.
