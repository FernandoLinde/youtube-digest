Making sure you saw our Argentina 101 report. Starting on page 69, we provide color on Argentine banks, including: (1) credit penetration, (2) where we are in the credit cycle, (3) margin and ROE evolution, and (4) deposits, among other topics. Hope you find it useful.

We like banks in the country (Macro and Galicia both OW), as we call for a gradual ROE recovery. Argentine banks are now trading at 1.2-1.3x P/BV, which looks asymmetrically positive. Our bear case is those names could trade at 1x book value with limited growth and real ROE returning to low-teens, while our base case in the model call for mid-high teens ROE ~18% and loan growth reaccelerating in line with loan-to-GDP penetration story. In this scenario we could see valuations back to 2.0x book. We discuss this and other details in our note “Argentine Banks: Positioning for a Multi-Year Turnaround - The Case for Buying on Weakness <https://jpmorgan.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12835026&ad=4073036062&h=Z_myaId&sig=JVnYdiRTkn9Zv56wK6w1jvMXTge&v=2&isig=MajkrNdRQbzv1mKxD5JzJ0bxOKt&url=https://markets.jpmorgan.com/research/email/scx/8ieene23/GPS-5171237-0/6f5da451-8737-4581-b339-4f49c64b6e48> ”.
