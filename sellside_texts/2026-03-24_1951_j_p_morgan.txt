We are reiterating our OW rating on Eve (EVEX US, ~72% owned by Embraer) and trimming once again our Dec-26 price target to US$6.00 vs US$7.00 previously, reflecting a more conservative approach towards eVTOL volumes ramp-up. Our price target is based on both DCF and multiple valuation. We remain OW on the stock due to: i) EVE’s business model continuous de-risking with its eVTOL accumulating over 28 flight tests (>1 hour of flight time); ii) Company maintains a leadership position with a potential backlog market share of 13% represented by LOIs totaling ~2.7k units valued at US$13.5bn at list prices plus US$1.6bn in services backlog; iii) Strong liquidity position of over US$540mn enough to fund operations until 2027 including undrawn credit lines vs an annual burn of ~US$250mn; and iv) Embraer continuous support on the development and certification of its eVTOL. EVEX is trading at 0.5x EV/Sales 2029e, compared to ACHR at 1.8x and JOBY at 7.8x. 

*	Flight test campaign momentum and de‑risking: Eve’s full‑scale, uncrewed engineering prototype completed its 1st flight on Dec. 19th and has since flown 28 times for a total of over 1h, with twice‑daily flights when weather permits; the program follows a disciplined, 4‑phase envelope expansion (hover, transition, cruise, and planned failures). Management plans ~300 flights in 2026, and expects 6 conforming prototypes to begin the flight portion of the certification campaign in 2027—an approach designed to accrue certification credits earlier and reduce cost and schedule risk relative to flight‑first paths. We see this continuous de-risk as an important trigger for the stock’s performance. 
*	Liquidity runway. Eve ended 2025 with a cash position of US$392mn and total liquidity of US$541mn, which includes undrawn BNDES standby facilities and grant availability. Subsequent financing actions further strengthened the runway, including a Dec-2025 EXIM‑backed facility (~US$15.6mn, US$13.6mn drawn), and a Jan-2026 US$150mn syndicated loan. Management reiterates funding sufficiency through 2028 despite a planned 2026 cash consumption of US$225–$275mn as program activity intensifies ahead of 2027 certification and entry into service. Based on 4Q25 liquidity post debt (US$362mn), we see Eve trading at 2.4x Market Cap/Net Liquidity, the lowest level since its listing and compared with a hist. average of 5.4x. 
*	Backlog views. Eve reports the largest and most diversified industry pipeline with ~2.7k eVTOL LOIs (non‑binding) across 27 customers with no single client representing >15% of total, complemented by conversion momentum of binding orders (up to 100 aircraft with pre‑delivery payments) and a Services & Support pipeline tied to ~1.1k aircraft that could generate ~US$1.6bn in revenue. 
*	Trimming Dec-26 PT to US$6.00. Reflecting a slower ramp-up eVTOL sales with 375 sales in both 2031 and 2032 vs 750 for both years prior, our Dec-26 PT moves to US$6.00 (from US$7.00 prior). Interesting to flag, according to our sensitivity, current screen price implies a market share of 5% and an EBITDA margin of 10% in a TAM of US$60bn in 2035.
