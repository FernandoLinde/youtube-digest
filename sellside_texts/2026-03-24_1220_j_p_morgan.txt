Top News from Past Week: 

*	<https://jpmorgan.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12845949&ad=4081029203&h=7qy8rUD&sig=7qFoRIDtBBREaxrIKXmiWQOmacw&v=2&url=https://valor.globo.com/empresas/noticia/2025/11/25/bndes-aprova-r-109-bilho-para-embraer.ghtml> Embraer APAC head bullish interview on Southeast Asia. Raul Villaron, Embraer's Senior VP of Marketing & Sales and Head of Asia Pacific, argued in an interview that Southeast Asia's island geography and gap between turboprops and narrowbody jets create ideal conditions for the E2 family. The E2 offers 30% lower trip costs than A320s or 737s with similar per-seat economics, making it viable for low-cost carriers to serve thin routes and replace aging turboprops. Villaron sees untapped growth potential across Indonesia, Philippines, Malaysia, and Vietnam, where right-sized aircraft can unlock new tourism destinations and connect underserved secondary cities – AeroTime (Mar. 20) <https://jpmorgan.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12845949&ad=4081029203&h=jt5XPv_&sig=EM0Ur8SsggLARAIpLTv6Cewr8E3&v=2&url=https://www.aerotime.aero/articles/embraer-apac-head-raul-villaron-on-why-company-is-betting-big-on-southeast-asia> . 
*	WEG announces IoC of R$420mn (~R$0.100 per share / ~0.2% yield). WEG (N-rated) announced the approval of IoC distribution totaling R$420mn, equivalent to ~R$0.100 per share representing a 0.2% yield as of the last closing price. The payment is scheduled for March 10th, 2027, with a net value of ~R$0.082 per share after the 17.5% withholding tax deduction. Shares will trade ex-interest on equity from March 23rd, 2026 onwards, and the amount will be credited toward mandatory dividends as per the company's bylaws – Press release (Mar. 17) <https://jpmorgan.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12845949&ad=4081029203&h=_TAAcfN&sig=XYlKZ88vzaXsp7UVEb9SQNxeRx6&v=2&url=https://www.rad.cvm.gov.br/ENET/frmExibirArquivoIPEExterno.aspx?NumeroProtocoloEntrega%3D1491274> . 
*	Tupy receives proposal for changes in fiscal council and board positions by minority shareholder. Tupy announced that Trígono Capital Ltda., asset manager that holds ~2.82% of the company’s share capital, submitted proposals including the nomination of 2 candidates for the Fiscal Council and a request to include the election of an alternate member for the Board of Directors in the agenda of the Annual General Meeting scheduled for April 30th, 2026. The Board of Directors will meet in the coming days to deliberate on the request regarding the alternate board member position – Press release (Mar. 19) <https://jpmorgan.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12845949&ad=4081029203&h=SgtSrM-&sig=GaKocE6Y189kffX3S0me7LmQweP&v=2&url=https://api.mziq.com/mzfilemanager/v2/d/5ab6a371-dd2f-452a-b6b2-22c97ed0abbb/3c6d89e6-383f-f039-a073-93b9409d9037?origin%3D2> . 
*	War has already caused airfare in Brazil to jump 17% yoy in March. The CEO of CVC Corp reported that airline ticket prices in Brazil increased by 17% yoy in March 2026, driven by the conflict in the Middle East and rising oil prices. Since February 28th, airlines have implemented 4 price hikes of 10% each, resulting in an effective increase of approximately 17% after adjustments and promotional discounts. CVC is monitoring the impact on demand in what is shaping up to be a complex year, marked by the World Cup, holidays, and elections. The industry remains on alert due to the high volume of flight cancelations to the Middle East, a key hub for connections to Asia – Valor Econômico (Mar. 19) <https://jpmorgan.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12845949&ad=4081029203&h=u8hxI4g&sig=W8yf6NmPjwW77YQl0bKHSvDkGQz&v=2&url=https://valor.globo.com/empresas/noticia/2026/03/19/guerra-ja-fez-passagem-aerea-no-brasil-saltar-17percent-em-marco-calcula-a-cvc.ghtml> .
