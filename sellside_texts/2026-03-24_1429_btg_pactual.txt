What to watch today - Steady prescription, softer pace: pharma growth remains resilient 


Read the full document here >> <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118896>  

Sindusfarma released February pharma industry sell-out data. The dataset, compiled monthly by IQVIA, a leading global pharmaceutical data and analytics provider, captures total pharmacy sales at pharmacy purchasing prices (PPP), covering all products sold through drugstores during the period, including both medications and non-pharma consumer goods. It is worth noting that the figures are not fully comparable due to calendar effects, with Carnival falling in February in 2026 versus March in 2025.

February sell-out grew +8.9% y/y, versus +11.3% in January and +14.5% in December. Generics growth also decelerated to +11.7% y/y in February, from +12.5% in January and +15.0% in December, but still points to healthy and consistent demand despite a softer pace relative to the year-end run rate.

As we mentioned in our recent report “Ain’t no mountain high enough <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118689> ”, the industry has historically been resilient, supported by strong physical distribution networks, recurring demand from chronic diseases, and a relatively fragmented market structure. Brazil’s pharma retail market reached roughly R$145bn in sales in 2025, growing around 9–10% annually over the past decade, driven by demographic trends such as population aging and rising prevalence of chronic conditions. Despite its size, the sector remains fragmented, with about 90,000 pharmacies nationwide, leaving room for continued consolidation led by large chains with stronger logistics, procurement, and compliance capabilities.

Overall, Sindusfarma data reinforce our constructive view on pharma retailers, supported by resilient Generics and prescription-drug demand, solid underlying growth fundamentals, and additional upside optionality from the future introduction of GLP-1 generics, which is not yet fully reflected in our estimates. We remain Buyers of the segment.
