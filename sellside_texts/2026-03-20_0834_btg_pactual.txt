Good 4Q figures 


Read the full document here >> <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118839>  

R$1.86bn in adj. EBITDA, 6% above us

Cemig delivered good 4Q results. Adj. EBITDA (ex. equity income) came in at R$1.86bn, down 3.5% y/y, but 6% above our estimate (R$1.75bn), with stronger-than-expected results from disco and G&T + trading, partially offset by weaker Gasmig figures. After lower-than-expected net financial expenses of R$290mn (vs. BTG’s R$400mn), equity income of R$41mn (vs. BTG’s R$18mn) and a R$788mn positive bottom-line impact from provision reversals related to post-employment health and dental plan obligations (vs. BTG’s R$858mn), net income reached R$1.88bn (vs. BTG’s R$1.93bn), or R$1.02bn when adjusted for non-cash/non-recurring items. Along with 4Q results, Cemig announced R$676mn in dividends (1.7% dvd. yield), bringing total dividends for 2025 to R$3.51bn, or a 9% dvd. yield. Leverage increased to 2.3x ND/adj. EBITDA (vs. 1.76x in 3Q25).

Distribution: lower bad debt expenses and flat energy losses

The distribution arm delivered adj. EBITDA of R$958mn, above our R$849mn estimate and up 4% y/y, driven by the Parcel B readjustment and lower bad debt expenses (-R$29mn vs. 4Q24). Billed volumes were down 1.4% y/y (vs. BTG’s +1.5% y/y), with lower demand from industrial, public services and concessionaire clients, partially offset by higher rural consumption. Energy losses remained flat q/q at 11.42%, below the regulatory threshold of 11.46%. In 2025, Cemig D opex reached R$4.66bn, 0.7% below its regulatory opex, while EBITDA reached R$3.72bn, 2.4% above its regulatory EBITDA.

Reducing short position at G&T, weaker results from Gasmig

The G&T + trading arms reported adj. EBITDA of R$750mn, above our R$689mn estimate, but down 19% y/y due to lower GSF (0.67 vs. 0.8 in 4Q24) and the negative impact of higher energy prices on the trading arm’s short position. The updated energy balance shows that Cemig reduced its short energy position to 3MWavg in 2026 (vs. 127MWavg in Aug-25), 68MWavg in 2027 (vs. 128MWavg) and 233MWavg in 2028 (vs. 326MWavg). Gasmig delivered EBITDA of R$184mn, below our R$191mn estimate, due to lower distributed volumes (-12% y/y).
