Current high‑conviction view: Is now the time to take profits? 


This year has begun strong—with over $6B of inflows into Brazil alone—and firm commodity prices have driven outsized gains in the most liquid names. At current valuation levels, we prefer to calibrate where upside has compressed or where multiples sit meaningfully above historical ranges and focus core exposure to the highest‑quality names. We recall that, even with recent global equity allocations into EM, positioning is low at only 5.6% (vs. 10-year avg of 6.5%), implying an additional ~$11B inflow to Brazil on a reversion to the average (see report here <https://jpmorgan.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12830977&ad=4070533253&h=5envhqt&sig=3XWUEtJ2BfxYCm4ece6uXGdXFxW&v=2&isig=2LSrIt0vXEmSZ1wup1b2UwuOzE5&url=https://markets.jpmorgan.com/research/email/scx/-4q8k7pd/GPS-5201832-0/d8dd839a-63e8-4dfd-951d-4f15002aca3e> ). Thus, we maintain a disciplined exposure to quality LatAm commodities, emphasizing operational efficiency and FCF returns. Within our coverage, our high-conviction ideas are: (a) for Oil & Gas: Petrobras, PRIO, Vista, and Vibra; (b) Metals & Mining: Vale and Aura; and lastly: (c) on Pulp & Paper: Suzano.
