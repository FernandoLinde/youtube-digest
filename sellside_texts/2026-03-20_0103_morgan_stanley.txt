This week's announcements reflect efforts from automakers to diversify revenue streams from cyclical auto end markets tethered to the uncertain fate of the consumer within a volatile market and attach to industries experiencing secular growth – see TSLA, GM, F, BWA, etc. 

On Tuesday, the US Department of Interior confirmed <https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/us-government-confirms-tesla-lg-energy-solutions-43-billion-battery-deal-2026-03-17/> that Tesla and LG Energy Solution (covered by Young Suk Shin) signed a $4.3bn battery supply agreement (~50-60 GWh on our estimate). This facility in Lansing, Michigan was originally meant to support General Motors' <https://investor.gm.com/news-releases/news-release-details/gm-sell-stake-lansing-battery-cell-plant-partner-lg-energy>  EV business, but was subsequently sold back to LG in December 2024 as a part of GM's capacity rationalization exercise. Production at the LFP battery cell plant is scheduled to begin next year and will supply Tesla's Megapack 3 energy storage systems produced in Houston.

In a separate announcement <https://financialpost.com/pmn/business-pmn/gm-pivots-tennessee-battery-plant-to-tap-energy-storage-boom> , Ultium Cells, a battery joint venture between General Motors and LG Energy Solution, will start production of LFP cells at its Tennessee plant next month in effort to revitalize factories idled by the EV demand slowdown. LG is retooling four other EV battery plants across North America to make LFP cells for stationary ESS, adding flexibility beyond EV battery production to stabilize demand.

These newly announced partnerships reinforce 3 key themes: 1) repurposing BEV capacity for ESS, 2) onshoring critical supply chains, and 3) lessening China supplier dependence.

1) The electrification landscape is shifting as OEMs increasingly redirect capital and capabilities from BEV to energy storage systems, while suppliers similarly look to diversify battery exposure beyond autos. We forecast <https://ny.matrix.ms.com/eqr/article/webapp/594f59ca-b8d3-11f0-a4e9-13dd0cf82def#/section=4> a 15.9mn 2026 US Auto SAAR, with ICE volumes +1% and BEVs -20% y/y – while autos is levered to the consumer with a wide variability in outcomes (2027 US SAAR bull case of 17.5mn vs. bear case of 14.5mn), energy demand has grown exponentially on the Powering AI theme. Ford is a notable example, entering the BESS market <https://ny.matrix.ms.com/eqr/article/webapp/7453c766-da1a-11f0-a3ae-2aac6342f563?ch=rp&sch=cc> with roughly $2bn of planned investment and targeting ~20 GWh of annual capacity from 2027. This aligns with our Global Utilities analysts’ forecast for stationary battery deployments to grow at a ~24% CAGR to ~875 GWh annually by 2030, before considering any incremental upside from data center co-location.
