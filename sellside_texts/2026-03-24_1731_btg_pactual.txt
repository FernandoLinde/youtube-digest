What If Margins Overshoot? Analyzing Margin, Working Capital, and ROIC Dynamics

Aimed for normalization, delivered an overshoot


While higher fuel prices could lead to a meaningful working capital expansion, we believe EBITDA margins in March (and potentially in April) could reach ~R$250-300/m³, therefore allowing fuel distributors to comfortably finance this step-up in working capital. In this context, ROIC could even expand, based on our estimates, as stronger EBITDA offsets higher invested capital. We expect both Vibra and Ultrapar’s Ipiranga to deliver EBITDA/m³ close to (or even above) R$200/m³ in 1Q26, with this dynamic potentially spilling over into 2Q26 and supporting strong earnings momentum. Still, we believe 1Q26 could bring negative cashflow generation, given the higher working capital needs - this makes us continue to prefer having fuel distribution exposure through Ultrapar, which also has a cheaper valuation (12.5x 2026E P/E). We also have a Buy rating on Vibra, which trades at 13.2x 2026E P/E.


We think EBITDA margins close to R$200/m³ in 1Q26 are likely


We believe the combination of a closed import window for diesel and regional players avoiding imports due to the wide gap between PBR and import parity prices (IPP) has resulted in a tighter-than-expected market, creating a favorable setup for incumbents in 1Q26. Based on our discussions with industry participants, this situation has been allowing for EBITDA/m³ margins of R$250-300/m³ during March, which could lead to EBITDA/m³ around R$200/m³ for Ipiranga and Vibra in 1Q26 - see Figures 2 and 3. Importantly, we see these elevated margins extending into April, as measures aimed at incentivizing imports (diesel subvention and potential ICMS exemption for imported diesel) are likely to take several weeks to impact supply. In our view, this two-month window of stronger profitability is sufficient to fund higher working capital requirements.


Higher margins could finance higher WK and allow for ROIC expansion


With fuel prices moving higher (e.g., PBR's diesel +12% YTD, Acelen's +72% YTD, import parity at +94% YTD), we expect a meaningful increase in working capital in 1Q26. However, we believe stronger margins in March/April should largely offset this effect. At these levels, EBITDA/m³ expansion could also drive ROIC upside. Assuming 1Q26 EBITDA/m³ of (i) R$197/m³ for Ipiranga, we estimate ROIC could reach 13.9% in 1Q26 and 15.6% in 2Q26 (vs. 13.1% in 2025), and (ii) R$213/m³ for Vibra, ROIC could expand to 15.2% in 1Q26 and 16.7% in 2Q26 (vs. 15.1% in 2025), as working capital stabilizes.


Resumption in diesel imports could hold back margins


On the other hand, the government is working on reducing import parity prices (IPP) in the domestic market, including zeroing ICMS on diesel imports, which could eventually lead to a more ample supply in Brazil. This, coupled with the R$0.32/liter subvention for diesel imports, could compress the import parity gap to around -5% vs. IPP, per our estimates, effectively restoring import arbitrage and rebalancing supply.

In such a scenario, competitive pressures would likely intensify, normalizing margins and reducing the current tightness in the domestic market. However, we reiterate: extraordinary results in March/April are enough to offset the impact of higher WK.
