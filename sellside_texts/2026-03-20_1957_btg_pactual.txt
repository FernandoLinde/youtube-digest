The bar was already low, but Q4 results were weaker

We were expecting a weak set of Q4 results for Tupy, but the company reported an even weaker print, mostly due to misses on both topline and margins. Accordingly, net revenue clocked in at R$2.2bn (-12% y/y; 6% below our estimate), while reported EBITDA reached -R$273mn. This was primarily due to R$312mn of non-recurring items, mostly related to (i) a R$325mn impairment in the period; (ii) R$72mn in restructuring costs and (iii) R$40mn regarding the reduction to realizable value of inventories. On the other hand, we note that these negative impacts were partially offset by a positive impact of R$174mn related to the sale of IPI tax credit claims through the assignment of rights. Adjusting for these effects, adj. EBITDA came in at R$39mn (-85% y/y, 55% below our estimate), implying a weak 2% margin (down 8.3p.p. y/y, 2.0p.p. below our estimate). Lastly, we estimate a adj. net loss of R$93mn (vs. BTG's -R$154mn estimate) for the period. 

Weaker volumes across the board, especially in domestic structural components 

Revenue in the Structural Components & Manufacturing Contracts division fell 17% y/y, with domestic revenue down 40% y/y (impacted mainly by weak heavy vehicle sales amid high interest rates and customers’ indirect exports) and external revenue down 5% y/y (due to lower sales of commercial vehicles and the uncertain environment related to tariffs). The Energy & Decarbonization segment posted a 23% y/y increase in revenue, with domestic revenue up 36% y/y (driven by new businesses and revenues from proprietary engines) and external revenue down 28% y/y. Lastly, revenue in the Distribution division increased 6% y/y (domestic up 13% y/y and external down 17% y/y). All in, domestic revenue fell 22% y/y to R$799mn, while external revenue declined 6% y/y to R$1.4bn.

ST story remains challenging and we prefer to stay on the sidelines; reiterate Neutral

Capex totaled R$177mn (vs. R$93mn in Q3), primarily related to new foundry and machining programs, initiatives aimed at improving operational efficiency, and synergies between operations, in addition to investments in health, safety, and environmental standards. Additionally, leverage increased q/q to 3.4x Net Debt/Adjusted EBITDA (from 2.6x in Q3), reflecting the weaker operating trend. Looking ahead, we expect investors to monitor (i) the impacts of lower tariffs on volumes in external markets, (ii) domestic automotive production trends, (iii) the ramp-up of manufacturing contracts, and (iv) the leverage and cash generation outlook. All in, we believe earnings momentum is likely to remain sluggish in the coming months, and until we gain greater visibility on earnings, we prefer to remain Neutral on the name.
