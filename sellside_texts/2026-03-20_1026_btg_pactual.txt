Cyrela is buying a land play from Helbor


Helbor announced today an MoU with Cyrela for the acquisition of a stake in an SPE (HESA 159), aiming to develop a low-income project (likely under the Vivaz brand) in the “Semp Toshiba” land plot (owned by HESA 159). After the transaction, which is subject to precedent conditions, Helbor will hold a 30% stake in HESA 159 (it currently owns 55%). In addition, the company also announced that Cyrela is acquiring ~19k CEPACs (Certificados de Potencial Adicional de Construção), in cash, held by the SPE.


Positive move, aiming to curb leverage; Buy rated (0.4x P/TBV)


We believe the move sends a positive message: Helbor seems committed to taking steps to curb leverage and increase asset turnover. However, we highlight that the immediate deleverage effects from this transaction should be limited, since: (i) we estimate that the company will collect ~R$40mn in cash upon closing; plus (ii) another ~R$250mn during the project, which may be launched in 2027 (as Helbor will retain a financial stake in the project, becoming a partner of Cyrela). We see this deal as positive, since the cash collection represents 16% of Helbor’s net debt, implying a relevant deleveraging (which we see as crucial for a stock re-rating). We have recently upgraded Helbor to Buy (see the report here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/109585> ), as the stock looks excessively discounted (0.4x P/TBV).
