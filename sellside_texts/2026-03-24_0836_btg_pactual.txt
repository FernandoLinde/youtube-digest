Quick take 


The bulls will hang on to the impressive sequential margin improvement in Brazil (F&P in Brazil -10bps YoY; nice job fending off higher beef), the proactive headcount reduction announcement with US$10m in annual savings, lower capex in 2025 (US$281m vs. low-end of guidance of US$300m), the higher dividend (17% increase YoY, implies 3.6% yield), and comments on lower delivery costs in Brazil. The bears will focus on continued topline weakness in Brazil (‘situation in Brazil will last a while’ … ‘monetizing market share advantage we have built over the years’…industry still seeing MSD traffic declines…goal is to reach inflation into 2Q and 2H). The 4Q result itself we’d qualify as a slight miss: PBT in-line after consider two positive one-offs related to Brazil taxes (20.5m above the line, 13.3 below the line) but EPS miss (US$25m vs. our 45m) due to an abnormally high tax rate in the Q. Trading at 10.5x P/E, 4.3x EV/EBITDA, we maintain our call on the basis of valuation though our sense today is 1Q won’t show any positive step-change in the topline story just yet.


4Q25: Brazil margin much better; NOLAD margins weaker


SSS grew 16% in 4Q (Brazil at 1.5% still sluggish, Mexico +5.6% but NOLAD only +1.7% implying continued weakness ex-Mexico, SLAD 49.5% - stronger, Argentina still doing well). Sales came 4% below us as SLAD sales were negatively impacted by Argie (ARS -30% YoY) but Arcos saw FX strength elsewhere in 4Q (BRL +8%, MXN +10%, CLP +2%, COP +14%). Adjusting for US$20.5m in one-off income related to taxes in Brazil and severance of US$8.7m in the Q, adj. EBITDA of US$152m came above our 145m and below consensus at 154m. Adjusted EBITDA growth was 14% on a 30bps margin expansion YoY (mainly driven by a sequential improvement in Brazil where EBITDA margin fell only 160bps YoY which is entirely explained by royalty hike, sequential improvement driven by F&P costs (10bps improvement) and mainly Payroll. Tax rate was 74% in 4Q (though still 38% FY25), making for a gnarly EPS miss (US$25m vs. our US$45m).


Capex clocks in lower than our lowered expectations; dividends above us


Management had originally guided for capex of US$300-350m for 2025. Earlier this year, alongside announcing 2026 guidance and 2025 final store openings (102 vs. guidance of 90-100), management noted that 2025 capex should come in at the low end of the guidance. 2025 capex was US$281m, below the US$300m low-end of the guidance. This is encouraging into a year with more efficient 2026 guidance

(US$275-325m). While we are a bit weary of lower productivity from new store openings, we do think there is something to the ‘doing more with less’ narrative management is spinning. The BoD declared dividends of US$0.28/share (vs. our US$0.25/share). This represents a 17% increase (vs. our 4%e), a function of lower capex and Brazil tax breaks.


We maintain our Buy rating


Our sense is that 2025 was a transition year in Brazil (weaker SSS on lower purchasing power: higher rates, affordability, sports betting + higher beef prices taking a toll on margins) and that the setup for the stock into 2026 is much better: stronger currencies, better FCF profile (on lower capex and Brazil tax savings), we are hopeful for better SSS and continued margin normalization in Brazil (don’t think 1Q will be a catalyst just yet). Any degree of normalization in Brazil beef prices and/or signs of an inflection point in Brazil SSS would be positive for the story but we think the stock does well on valuation (10.5x P/E and 4.3x EV/EBITDA; 15% FCFE yield ex-growth, 5% FCFE yield w growth) and stronger FX alone.
