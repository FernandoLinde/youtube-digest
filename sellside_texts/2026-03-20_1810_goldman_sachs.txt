LATAM Week Ahead: MPC Meetings in Mexico (-25bp to 6.75%) and Chile (Hold at 4.5%); MPC Minutes and MPR in Brazil; IPoM in Chile 
	
20 March 2026 | 5:04PM EDT | Research | Economics 	
	
	
[1] In Mexico, the central bank Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) will announce its rate decision on March 26 at 3:00pm EST. At the Feb 5 meeting the forward guidance turned clearly dovish, hinting that a rate cut would be considered at the March meeting (“looking ahead, the MPC will evaluate enacting additional rate cuts”). In the meantime, the central bank baseline scenario was upended by the conflict in the Middle East which led to a sharp increase in oil prices, strong USD, bear flattening of the US curve (with higher real rates reflecting a hawkish policy shock), rising risk-aversion, and tighter financial conditions. All amidst heightened global geopolitical risk.

A Hawkish Cut. We are leaning (barely so) towards a 25bp rate cut, to 6.75%, given the consistently dovish guidance and Banxico-speak, just mild MXN depreciation since the last meeting, significant tightening of domestic financial conditions, softening outlook for US and global demand, and the fact that, so far, the impact of the early excise tax and import tariff hikes seems to have been contained. Furthermore, the majority of the MPC directors do not seem overly concerned with the current inflation pressures and above-target inflation, choosing to focus instead on the soft growth outlook (large negative output gap) and the contribution to the inflation dynamics of a strong MXN. In addition, the risk of a major spike in fuel prices in the short term is contained by the fact that the government reached a voluntary 6-month agreement with gas stations to cap gasoline prices and has lowered the IEPS excise tax on fuel sales.

In case of a cut, we anticipate a split decision (with director Jonathan Heath voting for a hold) and the central bank raising the bar for another cut in May (hawkish cut).

Our degree of conviction that the central bank will cut is not high. In fact, we assess a high 40% probability of no cut in a nod to the ongoing oil price supply shock and heightened uncertainty. If the MXN/USD exchange rate keeps depreciating next week and Tuesday's first-half March core CPI is much higher than expected, the likelihood of holding a 7.00% rate will increase, eventually becoming the most probable outcome.

Since the last meeting MXN/USD weakened (to 17.96 from around 17.33 ahead of the Feb 5 meeting), oil prices (Brent) are up approximately 65% (Brent US$111.2 barrel), and 1- and 10-year UST rates moved by up by 39bp/+21bp to 3.80%/4.39%, and 5-year CDS spreads widened by 24bp to 109. Headline and core inflation expectations for 2026 deteriorated (+5/+6bp to 4.00%/4.17%) and the median expectation for growth in 20206 rose by 20bp to 1.50%.

The inflation backdrop remains challenging. Annual headline inflation is now tracking at 4.13%, above the upper-limit of the inflation target band. Tax and import tariff hikes pressured core-food in Jan, with the impact tapering during Feb. The normalization of non-core inflation from very low prints (perishable food prices in particular) is now driving headline inflation up; inflation among freely-determined prices accelerated to 4.60% yoy, with core inflation tracking at a high 4.48% yoy. In addition, high labor costs are likely to keep services inflation high in the 4%-handle
