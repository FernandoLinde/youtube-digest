Our take. Negative news. While we still have limited visibility on the practical implications from today's decree, our preliminary assessment suggests Petrobras will likely lose BRL 0.32/L competitiveness vs. other diesel players (importers and refineries), for an estimated negative FCF impact of USD 1.2bn in 2026 FY. We believe this measure increases uncertainty around timing for an eventual adjustment in diesel prices, especially when considering recent news flow <https://publishing.gs.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Fpublishing.gs.com%2FtrustedLink%3Fopentoken=T1RLAQIQa6RyuPxCBW6H5qqYdQzOX7StBBAZQNXFO8mWXHLlcxeb6wT5AACwDvH_Ee2a1KCTB4aCTjaJGtQMJp0povafIs2C9sDbdBKburOab993D7YUbAcjeACBHClg25G3sBgo46Rrm8dKtrJjOaOYMMz-E_4qdF8yEKcMiFsW1r97jotVwHXtO1SQPlVClw1Owg8WtG-qUr0NvTUUC27mUhFIcbER-nZep1ZfR_idT6zPIf01U_G6cBypfIy3oncX2n1crEd-VytFncnCADinlO02CiPw6ztK3gM*%26id=d1c282ef-c3d0-4fe8-971b-eeba331b2e4a%26utm_medium=pa/1/0100019d08e19131-968f03c5-74f7-496d-ba26-68e829cfa937-000000/yQFmGaHrcXiie5aDPLH1TispKaiyWYXhuLTjaeMfcaE=449>  around potential diesel shortages and the suspension from fuel auctions originally scheduled for early next week. 

The Brazilian government published today (March 19) a decree specifying the general rules and broader guidelines for the calculation of the diesel price reference which shall be used to determine at which price local producers and importers would have the right to receive the previously announced subsidy of BRL 0.32/liter. Recall, the government recently announced <https://publishing.gs.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Fpublishing.gs.com%2FtrustedLink%3Fopentoken=T1RLAQKR7eHjWH1-9Jl_RlnaS_MTZiqlkBBjIkJoSmWWxjf4_vyYZdXIAACwpe3LE4r6Nuk_Lp2lYgqTlAoBLGuB_sGOkNs2h_cowXUZjbtj1AA0HGJG0saLhKf7D64mEn0ZS6PJmrNLxMLwSI6Gd1jpviRS7G5toFcdMSInqF24PTnEqZMq3NLBuobOGcWKYIapkBFrVZqbcGCM78BXnPwJwqdYtBXGANIy0kvNo7VhOax3vzzdFdEIm-Tx-Bv7Ke0vDaT07VZw1jjjCJXfZkyFXuM6lyAbCtrMCkI*%26id=41301e7f-0854-424b-8f55-c122bf931e8f%26utm_medium=pa/1/0100019d08e19131-968f03c5-74f7-496d-ba26-68e829cfa937-000000/nx9Sm4tZmUTP05W1Rp1tZzIdaIzUlL8s24-KV72aEcw=449>  i) the reduction of federal taxes over diesel prices, and ii) a BRL 0.32/liter subsidy to refineries and importers, both measures aiming to dampen the effect of higher diesel prices for final consumers. 

According to the document <https://publishing.gs.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Fwww.in.gov.br%2Fweb%2Fdou%2F-%2Fdecreto-n-12.883-de-19-de-marco-de-2026-694120980/1/0100019d08e19131-968f03c5-74f7-496d-ba26-68e829cfa937-000000/KGDhVMyNjBeiCpLo7Oir849YLKBTdwGuSZ_pvklq5b4=449> , the reference price for i) diesel importers and ii) local producers which acquire oil from third parties; will consider import parity prices as benchmark. Meanwhile, the reference price for local producers which use their own oil volumes as input (Petrobras for instance) will consider their current diesel prices + BRL 0.32/liter as a benchmark. Those charging fuel prices in line with their respective reference price (with BRL 0.32/liter deducted) will be eligible to receive the subsidy.
