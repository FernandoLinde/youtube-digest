Brazil: Further Deterioration of 2026 Inflation Expectations; Lighter Selic Cuts in 2026 (Ramos) 
	
23 March 2026 | 7:40AM EDT | Research | Economics 	
	
	
Bottom Line: Inflation expectations for the policy-relevant 2026 rose 79bp to 4.17%, adding to the 19bp increase last week and moving further above the 3.0% inflation target midpoint. Inflation expectations for 2027/28/29 are now at 3.80%/3.52%(+2bp)/3.50%. We expect inflation expectations to deteriorate further in the coming weeks given the large oil price shock, and to start to also impact 2027. The median expectations for the Selic rate for end-2026/27/28 are now at 12.50% (+25bp) /10.50%/10.00%. The expectations for 2026/27 real GDP are at 1.84%(+1bp)/1.80%. The median expectation for the primary fiscal balance of 2026/27/28 remains in negative (deficit) territory, contrary to the government’s targets of non-negative prints, attesting to the low credibility and weak anchoring effect of the fiscal framework. The nominal fiscal deficit is expected to remain deep in the red (over 7%) over the medium term: 8.50%/8.00%/7.55%/7.27% for 2026/27/28/29.

DETAILS:

1. 

According to the central bank’s weekly survey of market analysts, the median inflation expectation for the policy relevant 2026 rose 7bp to 4.17% (adding to the 19bp increase last week and further above the 3.0% target). We expect inflation expectations to deteriorate further in the coming weeks given the large oil price shock, and to start to also impact 2027.

2. 

The median inflation expectations for 2027/28/29 are now at 3.80%/3.52%(+2bp)/3.50%.

3. 

The median market expectations for Selic for end-2026/27/28 are now at 12.50% (+25bp)/10.50%/10.00%.

4. 

The expectation for real GDP growth in 2026 is tracking at 1.84% (1.83% a week ago), and for 2027 at 1.80% (1.80% a week ago).

5. 

The median expectation for the primary fiscal balance remains in the red through 2028: -0.50%/-0.40%/-0.26% of GDP, contrary to the government’s path of rising primary surpluses. This is consistent with continuously rising public sector debt.

6. 

The BRL/USD median expectation for year-end 2026 is at 5.40 (5.40 a week ago) and for year-end 2027 at 5.45 (5.47 a week ago).
