Embraer announced this morning (link here <https://jpmorgan.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12842665&ad=4079314958&h=HjOtaCA&sig=FTEDBS83K8UwlGN3oXkDGVcwT92&v=2&url=https://www.embraer.com/media-center/en/?mediatype%3DNEWS%26detail%3D24788-Finnair-selects-Embraer-E195-E2%252C-ordering-up-to-46-aircraft> ), despite market skepticism regarding new orders due to current geopolitical tensions, that it has obtained a new order from Finnair of up to 46 E195-E2 aircraft, divided into 18 firm orders, 16 options and 12 purchase rights. We estimate the firm order portion totals around US$1.5bn at list prices, or US$1.0bn assuming a 35% discount. The firm order portion will be added to 1Q26 backlog and deliveries will start in 2H27, with the aircraft having a configuration of 134 seats. Worth mentioning that the E2-195 is up to 35% more efficient than the previous E190 operated by Finnair; therefore, helping the company to save on fuel and lower emissions. Post this order, Embraer's backlog in 1Q26 will be US$31.4bn with commercial backlog at US$15.2bn (assuming revenues of US$278mn in commercial in 1Q26 and US$1.3bn in aggregate). We expect a positive reaction as the stock is down 15% YTD vs IBOV at +10%. Additionally, investors’ sentiment has deteriorated since the beginning of the US-Israel-Iran war due to concerns regarding backlog health and the delay in potential new orders. We see EMBJ trading at 8.5x EV/EBITDA 2026e vs BA at 35.8x, BBD/B at 12.3x and AIR at 10.2x. 

*	First order of 2026: to bring back investor confidence in the name. Finnair's order marks the first order of 2026 and compares with orders of 15 aircraft in the same period last year – ANA’s order in February. In our view, this order is important to improve investor sentiment on the name as the most asked question in the past 20 days was: What are the risks regarding deliveries and backlog due to rising oil prices and geopolitical tensions? Based on the last 12 months orders, book -to-bill is at 2.8:1 (delivered of 79 during 2Q25-1Q26 and orders of 224 aircraft). Keep in mind that the difference between options and purchase rights is the level of airline commitment, with puchase rights having no guarantee and often no pre-determined slot. 
*	About Finnair. Finnair is Finland’s flag carrier founded in 1923, operates a network connecting Asia, North America, and Europe via its Helsinki hub, with year‑round direct flights to Lapland; it’s a 15‑time Skytrax “Best Airline in Northern Europe,” a member of the oneworld alliance, majority state‑owned (55.9%), and dominates Finnish air travel. Its fleet totals 80 aircraft, including 12 Embraer E190s.
