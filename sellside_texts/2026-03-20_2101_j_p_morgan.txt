Tupy released its 4Q25 results (link here <https://jpmorgan.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12827815&ad=4067988175&h=8I2Ua9k&sig=Uv7gm9kxXLn6WSVPJgy5k3cagT7&v=2&url=https://www.rad.cvm.gov.br/ENET/frmExibirArquivoIPEExterno.aspx?NumeroProtocoloEntrega%3D1492682> ) on Mar. 19th after market close. It was another quarter of dismal results as adj. EBITDA missed JPMe and consensus by 63% and net income was negative at R$627mn vs expectations of a loss of ~R$50mn. Excluding ~R$545mn in one-offs (impairments, restructuring costs and others), net income would have been -R$80mn, still worse than expected. Weakness in heavy vehicle volumes in the automotive segment (sales -17% yoy) and an impairment of R$325mn were the main culprits for the lackluster quarter. We see TUPY trading at 4.3x EV/EBITDA 26e vs POMO at 4.5x and RAPT at 4.6x. 

*	Our take. Despite the stock’s underperformance at -6% YTD vs IBOV at +12%, we expect a negative reaction in tomorrow’s trading session given the higher than expected loss of R$627mn. Moreover, the stock’s RSI remains at ~36, above the 30 threshold deemed as oversold, and there is limited visibility on earnings improvements (i.e. NA Class 8 truck build rates down ~35% YTD). 
*	Main positives. i) Net revenues were at R$2.18bn, -1% vs JPMe but in-line with consensus; ii) SG&A was R$228mn, -4% vs JPMe and representing 10.4% of revenues, -0.4pp vs our call and -0.6pp yoy; and iii) Operational FCF was positive despite the weak bottom line at R$358mn (-40% yoy) with working capital down 8 days qoq and down 12 days yoy, mostly driven by a reduction in inventories (we need to understand how sustainable this is). 
*	Main negatives. i) Gross margin was at 8.0%, -8.9pp yoy and -3.9pp vs JPMe; iii) Adj. EBITDA margin was at 1.8%, -3.0pp vs JPMe and -8.3pp yoy, also being 4.0pp below implied consensus of 4.9%; iv) Net financial cost was at -R$92mn, 46% worse than our call; and v) Income tax was at -R$168mn vs -R$23mn a year ago, impacted by tough comps given a tax recovery that happened a year ago. 
*	Revenue breakdown. Domestic market represented 37% of 4Q top-line, and the foreign market the remaining 63%. In terms of segment breakdown: i) Automotive segment revenues (80% of top-line) were at R$1.75bn (-17% yoy) and flat vs. JPMe; ii) Energy & Distribution revenues (10% of top-line) were R$219mn (+23% yoy), +1% vs. JPMe; and iii) Distribution revenues (10% of top-line) were at R$215mn (-6% yoy), +6% vs JPMe. 
*	Other highlights: i) The Adjusted EBITDA margin of the traditional business, which includes structural components and hydraulic products, was at -1% vs MWM at +10%; ii) The appreciation of the BRL and MX peso against the USD had a negative impact of R$70mn; iii) Lower production levels also affected quality indicators, resulting in negative impact of R$57mn in 4Q; iv) Capex was at R$177mn, +27% vs JPMe and +1% yoy; and v) During 4Q, Tupy sold IPI future tax credits in the amount of R$174mn (tax benefit related to exports carried out between 1988 and 1990).
