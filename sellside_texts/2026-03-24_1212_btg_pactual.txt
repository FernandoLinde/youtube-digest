	4Q25 conference call highlights  

 

Cyrela’s conference call has just ended. Please find below the main highlights.

 

Overall, management highlighted that 2025 was a challenging year for the housing sector, mainly due to a tougher macro backdrop. Nevertheless, they reiterated that Cyrela delivered a strong (and resilient) operating performance throughout the year, while financials also remained healthy and indebtedness ended the year at a manageable level. They also noted that 2026 should be another challenging year, but Cyrela is ready to navigate market conditions and has a resilient platform. We remain BUYers of Cyrela, with the stock trading at 5.5x P/E 2026E.

 

Main highlights:

 

*	Inventory levels: The CEO mentioned that sales speed has indeed decreased in 2025 compared to 2023 and 2024 (which saw above-average performance). That said, Cyrela will not revise its launch pipeline for 2026, although it acknowledges that inventory levels are higher.

 

*	Cyrela’s stake in Cury: With Fábio Cury transitioning from the CEO role, Cyrela will leave its two seats on the Board. The CEO mentioned that Cyrela’s participation was not as accretive and that there could be conflicts of interest due to its exposure to Vivaz. Despite that, Cyrela does not plan to fully divest from Cury in the ST. There are also no specific plans regarding Lavvi and PLPL.

 

*	Revenue dynamics: The CFO explained the changes in Cyrela’s revenue recognition rule in Q4. He mentioned that Cyrela will now decide at the end of each quarter which projects it is certain will not be canceled and will therefore start recognizing these revenues. Looking ahead, there is still a gap to be closed in terms of accrued and deferred revenues.

 

*	SP Zoning Law: The city hall and the municipal council are attempting to challenge the measure with solid arguments and expect it to be suspended soon. They cannot provide a timeline, but they do not work with a scenario in which the measure remains in place for a longer time.

 

*	Gross margins at Vivaz: The CFO stated that they expect a recovery in gross margins for their low-income brand (Vivaz). When looking at launches, Vivaz’s margins are slightly above the 36% level, suggesting room for further improvement.

 

*	Deliveries in 2026: Cyrela expects deliveries to grow ~15% y/y in 2026, of which 85% are sold and 49% collected so far. For 2027, volumes should be similar, with 86% sold and 34% collected. Vintages are well sold and healthy in terms of LTV.

 

*	Changes in MCMV: Regarding potential changes in MCMV, the CFO said that the likelihood of approval is high and should be positive for the company.

 

*	Landbank: Cyrela remains highly selective in landbank acquisitions and avoids providing a specific budget for this line in 2026. Growth for the sake of growth is not the focus; if projects deliver strong ROEs, they will continue acquiring land. The CEO also noted that Vivaz is still growing and should continue to do so.

 

*	Inflation costs: It is still too early to assess potential construction cost increases related to geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, but so far the company has not felt any impact.

 

*	Priorities for 2026: The main challenge is to sustain ROE levels. The CEO highlighted that the year should be quite challenging, especially considering the growth in operations over recent years.

 

*	SG&A: Operations have expanded significantly, with larger launches in 2025 (e.g., higher marketing expenses), especially at CashMe (which saw strong origination growth during the year). In addition, higher sales at Vivaz also bring incremental costs, such as ITBI and registration expenses.

 

*	“Other results” line: The CFO noted that the main changes in this line came from the amortization of goodwill from the Eden project (acquired via quotas) and an impairment in an older SPE in São Paulo.

 

*	Changes to the workweek: The CFO said there is nothing concrete yet and that they would need to assess the exact terms of any legal changes to properly estimate the impact.

 

*	FCF in Vivaz: Management noted that Vivaz has been able to grow while generating cash, as sales speed at launches remains strong, and transfers to banks are also quick (supporting FCF generation). At the consolidated FCF level, growth in the mid/high-end segment weighed on performance this year, but Vivaz continues to perform well.

 

*	1Q26 performance: The CFO noted that it is still too early to comment on sales performance, but launches were lower y/y QTD, while inventory sales are showing a modest increase.

 

*	Tax reform: Management sees a negative impact for the mid/high-income segment, but a positive one for MCMV. Mandatory adoption of the VAT model applies only to launches from 2029 onward, and the company will assess whether it makes sense to adopt it earlier.

 

*	Vivaz’s share in operations: They highlighted Vivaz + JVs account for ~40% of the company’s results. The company expects this to keep growing, although it is difficult to estimate its exact share going forward, given the uncertainty around the mid/high-end segment over the next three years.
