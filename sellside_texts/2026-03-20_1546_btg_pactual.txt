YDUQS: New share buyback program announced

       

Earlier this morning, YDUQS announced a new R$100mn share buyback program (up to ~9.9mn shares, ~4% of free float) to be executed over the next 18 months. The company’s previous buyback program was concluded in February 2025, under which ~30.5mn shares were repurchased (~75% of the program), totaling roughly R$300mn. See below the currently active buyback programs across our coverage.

 

The announcement follows last week’s results (see our review <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118016> ), which were somewhat weak, with free cash flow landing near the low end of guidance (~R$500mn). For 2026, management expects FCF to be broadly stable or slightly higher y/y, with capital allocation focused on selective M&A and deleveraging (targeting ~1.0x ND/EBITDA by end-2027). The possibility of a buyback had already been flagged, making the announcement broadly expected.

 

All-in, we view the program positively as it signals confidence in the medium-term cash generation outlook and adds flexibility to shareholder returns, alongside ongoing deleveraging.

 

We maintain our Buy rating, backed by a still compelling ~18% 2026 FCF yield, although risks to our estimates have increased as early 2026.1 intake trends point to a ~7% y/y decline, with limited visibility on a rebound.
