Blau (N-rated) stands at a pivotal juncture, leveraging a robust pipeline of high-complexity and biological products, but facing meaningful execution risks and market uncertainties. In this report, we refresh our MT top-line modeling, applying differentiated probabilities to pipeline delivery to reflect risks around timing, product success, and capex overruns. As pipeline execution evolves, we plan to revisit our framework, but at present, we are delaying some deliveries versus prior estimates. Our revised model forecasts a good 5y top-line and EPS CAGR of 15%. However, ST momentum remains limited due to a softer public market and a likely tougher private environment. At 8.4x/8.0x 2026E/2027E P/E and with limited upside to our revised Dec’26 PT of R$13.00/sh (from R$14.50), we see risk-reward as balanced, estimating a fair forward multiple at 10x while low share liquidity remains a key constraint. We remain Neutral on Blau. 

*	Revisiting our MT top-line forecast. Blau’s pipeline, guided through 2032E, targets a market of ~R$14bn, on top of the current R$10.4bn TAM where Blau holds ~20% share. The pipeline splits into synthetic products (2025-32E) and monoclonal antibodies (+2028E), with the latter carrying higher execution risk. Adjusting for delivery risk and market growth, we estimate an incremental TAM of ~R$10.9bn, and a fair value of R$13.0/sh assuming a 25% terminal market share. In a full delivery scenario, Blau could be worth R$16.5/sh, by our estimates. 
*	On a risk-adjusted basis, Monoclonal Antibodies have limited impact on mid-term P&L but could be transformational if fully delivered. On a risk-adjusted basis, mAbs have limited mid-term P&L impact, reaching just 4% of consolidated top-line by 2028E, but potentially ramping to 20% (R$1.04bn) by 2035E as new technologies come online. If fully successful, mAbs could be transformational, driving significant upside to sales and EBITDA. 
*	ST momentum is likely soft. Short-term performance is constrained by limited pricing power, as reflected in the IPM-H index (-0.3% y/y in 4Q25, -0.9% in January), and a challenging market environment. We forecast 2026E net revenues of R$2.0bn (+16% y/y, but 3% below our prior estimate), with sequential gross margin improvements to 40.4% in 2026E (+90bps y/y), supported by better mix and operational efficiency.
