A healthy dose of momentum

Solid SSS growth, boosted by GLP-1s and branded drugs

Panvel reported a solid set of Q4 results tonight, broadly in line with our estimates, showcasing strong SSS growth, solid operating performance (with another round of margin gains), and healthy cash flow generation. As already anticipated by the company during its Investor Day in February (see more in our report Dose of ambition: unveiling bold LT guidance <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118251> ), retail sales grew 18% y/y to R$1.67bn (1% below our estimate), reflecting SSS growth of 14.7% y/y (vs. 9.1% in 3Q25 and our +13%) and MSSS growth of 11.6%. Productivity rose 13% y/y to R$849k/store/month, while market share in the South region increased 70bps y/y to 13.9%. As a result, consolidated gross revenues grew 16% y/y, below retail growth due to the phase-out of its wholesale division, reaching R$1.68bn. This was largely driven by the growth in branded drug sales, up 21% y/y (helped by solid GLP-1 momentum), followed by generics (+19% y/y), HPC (+16% y/y), and OTC (+12% y/y).

Adjusted EBITDA up 28%; net profit grew 35% y/y

Gross profit reached R$503mn, up 19% y/y (2% above us), with a margin of 29.8% (+50bps y/y), mostly explained by the discontinuation of lower-margin wholesale operations. Adjusting for R$21mn in profit sharing/bonuses provisions and R$2mn in one-offs, adjusted EBITDA (pre-IFRS16) reached R$105mn (in line), up 28% y/y, with a margin of 6.2% (+50bps), as SG&A remained virtually flat y/y as a percentage of net revenues. After accounting for financial results (-R$12mn vs. -R$7mn a year ago), net income (pre-IFRS16) came in at R$45mn (+35% y/y; vs. our R$50mn estimate, on slightly higher D&A).

A welcome FCF print; leverage down to 0.9x ND/EBITDA

On the back of lower capex (-31% y/y to R$34mn), better operating results, and a healthier cash cycle (down 9 days y/y, mostly due to improved inventory dynamics), FCF reached R$42mn (vs. -R$30mn a year ago). After accounting for interest payments, net debt stood at R$287mn (vs. R$311mn last quarter), with leverage declining to 0.9x ND/EBITDA (vs. 1.0x in 3Q25).

Resiliency + attractive valuation + momentum = BUY

We continue to see decent upside for Panvel as a resilient pharma player with solid momentum, and Q4 was another testament to that. Our positive view on the company is based on four pillars: (i) high store density in its core market, creating a natural barrier to entry and supporting continued expansion into other cities within the same region; (ii) room for margin gains, including a structurally stronger profitability profile following the divestment of the distribution business; (iii) strong private label penetration (+1.2k active SKUs), supported by a solid multichannel proposition (28% of retail sales via online channels); and (iv) an attractive valuation (12x P/E 2026). We reiterate our BUY.
