Cyrela hosted today its 4Q25 conference call with top management. Please see below for the main highlights. We see CYRE trading at 6.3x P/E 26e and 1.2x P/BV vs. EZTC at 7.3x/0.76x. 

*	Outlook 1Q. Sales speed is accomodating being below previous years; however, it is still good enough for Cyrela to continue to launch and be profitable. At this point there is no need to revise launches downward. Too soon to comment on presales in 1Q, but the company had lower launches in Jan+Feb. vs 2025, but sales of inventories are growing yoy. 
*	Revenue recognition. There was no change in the percentage of completion rules; Cyrela is now evaluating every quarter which projects the company will not give up or not, allowing the company to start their revenues recognition and this will continue going forward. Cyrela revenues to be recognized are over R$11bn. Additionally, CYRE expects in 2026 ~R$5.5-6.0bn in revenues from construction evolution vs ~R$5.0bn in 2025. 
*	São Paulo permits. The resumption in permit issuance should happen soon. Cyrela’s base case is that the market will normalize soon and at this point there was no impact on the company’s figures. 
*	Margin breakdown by segment. Vivaz gross margin is improving, reaching 36% in 4Q vs 34% in the full year; therefore, margins should be closer to 36% in the future vs 34% today. Consolidated gross margin should continue to improve, being around 34%. 
*	Inventory levels in SP. Sales speed was better in the past, but it still healthy for good companies. 
*	Improvement in MCMV. Looking at this matter thru the sector association, and Cyrela expects improvements to be approved and have a positive impact for the company. Cyrela never assumes any improvements on its viability studies. 
*	Deliveries. 2027 expected deliveries are 86% sold as of 4Q and 34% collected. 
*	Low income exposure. Around 30% at the organic level and over 40% including JVs. The mix in the future will depend on how fast mid and high income grow. 
*	Land bank. Cyrela will remain selective in land bank acquisiton, focused on high quality land plots. 
*	Launches. Vivaz (low income brand) continues to grow since the company is still small in the São Paulo market. 
*	Raw material prices. Overall, it is too early to comment on the imapct of higher oil prices on raw materials. 
*	Strategic focus for 2026: There are several challenges ahead as in every year, and Cyrela will continue to be focused on being profitable with a high ROE above 20%. 
*	Change in working scale. End of 6x1 is not defined yet, but it is too soon to say what the impact would be. 
*	Vivaz growth vs FCF. Vivaz is growing and generating FCF. However, growth in the mid-income segment has consumed part of the cash. 
*	IVA impact. Negative for mid and high and positive for low income. It will be mandatory for 2029 launches onwards.
