Truck fleet renewal in Brazil: a new policy to address an old challenge

In 2024, Brazil’s truck fleet totaled about 2.3 million vehicles, with an average age of slightly more than 12 years. The large share of aging trucks in Brazil increases carbon emissions, reduces productivity, and negatively affects road safety. This report focuses on the environmental impact of Brazil’s large and aging truck fleet, as well as on a new program designed to accelerate fleet renewal.

Move Brasil: a program to renew and modernize Brazil’s truck fleet

The Move Brasil program is a nationwide initiative aimed at renewing and modernizing Brazil’s truck fleet in order to improve road safety, reduce emissions, and increase the efficiency of road freight transport. The program supports the replacement of older trucks through R$10 billion in financing funded by the National Treasury and the Brazilian Development Bank (BNDES). The financing covers new trucks produced in Brazil and used trucks manufactured from 2012 onward. When financing the purchase of a new truck, the program requires that an older vehicle be removed from circulation and dismantled. In the first two months of the project, 4,620 credit operations have already been approved, totaling R$5bn in financing.

Fleet renewal helps, but structural challenges remain

Addressing emissions from freight transport in Brazil may require a broader strategy beyond fleet renewal. While replacing older trucks with more efficient models can reduce emissions per vehicle, the overall environmental impact of the sector also depends on the structure of the transport system. Policies that encourage greater use of rail and water transport, combined with improvements in intermodal logistics and the increasing use of biofuels, could play an important role in achieving more substantial and long-term reductions in emissions.
