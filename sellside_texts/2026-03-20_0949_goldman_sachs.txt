Mexico: Real GDP Firmed During 4Q2025 Driven by Domestic Demand; Negative Contribution from Net Exports (Ramos) 
	
20 March 2026 | 8:43AM EDT | Research | Economics 	
	
	
Bottom Line: Real GDP growth accelerated to 0.86% qoq sa during 4Q2025, driven by robust household consumption (1.0% qoq sa), gross fixed investment (0.7% qoq sa) and also government consumption (0.8% qoq sa), mitigated by the negative contribution from net exports. The variation of inventories added a minor 10bp to quarterly growth, and the statistical discrepancy contributed with 1.8pt. On the supply side, the 4Q25 expansion of real activity was driven by the secondary (0.9% mom sa) and labor-intensive services (0.9% qoq sa) sectors. Within the secondary sector we highlight soft manufacturing (0.1% qoq sa, vs -1.2% qoq sa during 3Q) and the rebound in construction (+4.3% qoq sa, from -2.5% qoq sa in 3Q25) activity. Mining activity increased by 1.5% qoq sa, second positive print in ten quarters. The statistical carry-over for 2026 is at 0.8%. Overall, real GDP growth decelerated to 0.6% in 2025, from the already lackluster 1.4% in 2024, with household consumption up 1.1% and GFI down 6.3%. Despite higher US tariffs lingering and the uncertainty exports performed well (7.0%) exceeding the annual growth of imports (3.1%).

Going forward, activity is expected to face headwinds from soft business confidence and peaking credit cycle. On the positive side, generous minimum wage increases, and a resilient labor market should offer some support to domestic demand. Investment is expected to continue to be impacted by both domestic and external uncertainty, in particular related to trade-policy direction in the US and the ongoing USMCA review.

DETAILS:

1. 

Real GDP grew 0.86% qoq sa during 4Q2025; a clear rebound from the 0.07% qoq sa Q3 variation. On an annual basis real GDP rose 1.8% yoy in 4Q2025, firming from -0.1% yoy 3Q25 print.

2. 

The economy grew a modest 0.6% in 2025, down from the lackluster 1.4% in 2024.

3. 

Final domestic demand firmed to +0.9% qoq sa in 4Q25, from 0.7% qoq sa in 3Q25. Private consumption grew a solid 1.0% qoq sa in 4Q25 (vs 1.3% qoq sa in 3Q25), government consumption 0.8% qoq sa, and gross fixed capital formation 0.7% qoq sa (vs -0.9% qoq sa in 3Q25), with private investment up 0.6% qoq sa (-0.5% qoq sa in 3Q25) and public investment up 2.0% qoq sa (-3.3% qoq sa in 3Q25).

4. 

Net exports' contribution to sequential growth was negative: -2.0pt, vs -1.9pt in Q3, given the underperformance of exports (+1.1% qoq sa) vis-à-vis the buoyancy of imports (+5.16% qoq sa). The variation of inventories added a minor 0.1pt neutral to 4Q sequential activity, and the statistical discrepancy added 1.8pt.

5. 

On the supply side, the 4Q25 expansion of real activity was driven by the secondary (0.9% qoq sa) and labor-intensive services (0.9% qoq sa) sectors, mitigated by the contraction of the low-weight primary sector (-1.4% qoq sa). Mining activity increased by 1.5% qoq sa, second positive print in ten quarters.
