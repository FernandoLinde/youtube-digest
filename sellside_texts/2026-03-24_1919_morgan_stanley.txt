WHAT'S CHANGED?	 
Dick's Sporting Goods (DKS.N)	 
Bull Case Valuation	 
From:   $300	 To: $335	 
	 As DKS is scaling its experiential formats, integrating FL, and pushing into higher-margin alternative revenue streams, we explore the bull case of one of the more compelling stories in retail. Remain OW, PT $250. 

DKS has one of the most asymmetric risk/reward skews in our coverage, with upside optionality on the "core" + Foot Locker (FL) and limited downside, shaped by negative imputed value from FL (~$43/share). Since Liberation Day (April 2, 2025), the stock has underperformed the S&P 500 by ~22% (NTM P/E compressing to ~13.1x from ~15.7x in February 2025), reflecting FL acquisition angst and a potential deceleration in core. As DKS is on the cusp of scaling its experiential formats and of its FL transformation, we explore the bull case to frame the potential upside.

1. Raising our bull case valuation. We are raising our bull case valuation by ~12% to $335, reflecting 1) higher EPS power from experiential retail (core), alternative revenue streams (GameChanger and retail media), and FL, and 2) a ~2 turn increase in our implied multiple to ~18.5x FY’27 P/E, consistent with "well-positioned" hardline retailers. This higher bull case improves our risk/reward, shifting the upside/downside to ~76%/~13% in our bull/bear cases.

In our bull case, we assume FY’28e revenue of ~$25.2bn (~6% above the Street), EBIT of ~$2.5bn (~12% above consensus), and an EBIT margin of ~9.8% (~55 bps above Street). We think the market is underappreciating DKS’s ability to grow physical foot traffic, expand a loyal customer base, and capture greater wallet share through its sports ecosystem, supporting our view that DKS will be one of the most enduring omnichannel retailers in the future. Risks to our new bull case include slippage in core DKS and a slower ramp in FL.

2. $20 x 20 P/E? We see a bull case path to ~$20+ EPS by FY’28e and believe a 20× P/E is attainable given the scarcity value and strategic importance of the DKS ecosystem. Unlike sporting goods competitors (e.g., ASO), which rely on traditional loyalty card or rewards programs, DKS has access to a fully integrated ecosystem that drives traffic, loyalty, and engagement. Our bull case scenario assumes core + alternative EBIT margin expands from ~11% in FY’25 to ~12%+ by FY’28e, while FL’s EBIT margin recovers from roughly -1% to ~5% over the same period. In this “winner-takes-most” environment, retail valuations have bifurcated, with the market assigning low- to mid-teens multiples to names where long-term growth and durability is questioned, while scaled winners with consistent growth and stronger competitive positioning command high-teens to low-20s.

3. What's new in our analysis: 1) a bottom-up, store-level profit build that captures the positive mix shift toward experiential formats and its margin implications, 2) accretion from high-margin GameChanger and retail media (45-70% EBIT margins that don't burden the traditional P&L), and 3) a deep dive into FL's historical gross margin, tracing the drivers of erosion and outlining a path for recovery. Our work points to higher core earnings power and margin potential than previously assumed, reinforcing our raised target multiple and bull case valuation.

4. Secular sports momentum and ecosystem strength. Our analysis is grounded in a constructive outlook on a Sporting Goods category that has grown ~7% since 2015, and in DKS’s ability to continue gaining share (currently ~4% of Total Sports Equipment PCE) by delivering greater value to suppliers and customers (athletes).

5. Category mix shift driving merchandise margin expansion. DKS continues to benefit from its rising penetration of the higher-margin footwear category, which has increased to ~28% of FY’24 product mix from ~20% in FY’15, supporting mix-driven merchandise margin expansion within the broader destination-retail strategy.

6. Destination retail enabling EBIT expansion. The case for experiential retail is twofold: 1) it brings the utility and enjoyment of sports into the store, transforming locations from transactional spaces into destinations and 2) it aligns with athletes’ drive to improve through hands-on product interaction within an athletic environment. This supports higher store productivity and a stronger merchandise offering, providing line of sight to ~115+ bps of EBIT margin upside in our bull case as the model scales and mix shifts toward experiential formats.
