US Tariff Impact Tracker: Imports Two Weeks Look Set to Pick Up Post LNY 
	
23 March 2026 | 5:01AM EDT | Research | Equity 	
	
	
US Tariff Impact Tracker – this past week, laden vessels from China to USA were up sequentially (+4% WoW) but still lower on a YoY basis (-17% YoY). Data suggests TEUs coming into Port of LA will be up next week (+2.5% WoW) following this past week's sequential decrease (-24%), before seeing a marked step-up at +24% WoW two-weeks out. We believe the WoW pickup over the next couple of weeks reflect the restart of imports following LNY and perhaps trying to get goods in while we know the tariff situation. As we enter into April we should start to have a better picture of import demand as we move beyond LNY.

Trade uncertainty remains prevalent in global trade, especially following the recent Supreme Court ruling and the subsequent announcement from the administration for new tariffs under Sec. 122 (see our macro team's assessment <https://publishing.gs.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Fpublishing.gs.com%2FtrustedLink%3Fopentoken=T1RLAQIyAheaS8ZgebU0LXxZo4v92OkVYhAgXwTpRQjgI9g_sz2BKgDSAACwJ4FeHVK4CITvLkoT7xvP29DuOm1S6y3A1kbEMzC_E7HacQEiYSt94M9LKc4Bx9Re1nikjl1ApgdVMBMcA_7uJEjpeQwARmkKHYGJ3Qt1Ya_E31h7C9tzHkfI-MQfBrC-gmjKRMq7kao-OjU7_wl4NKWfS8z8EwbJpmXHVGMJ6OOtpcFSdtyacTiXmtv0T3998v9QLTjmtXVyjcuqpmN6h3240F3_WhzgCfhPGhapkwA*%26id=7d7e8eac-67ef-46f6-89d1-5f56681e1ccb%26utm_medium=pa/1/0100019d19f0bba8-b3e0e188-89d1-4390-811f-e08b7ed8f6dc-000000/Rsiu6RqXPZewbQ6BwsHKFfezzT6EBSqhHw4BYkiIf6Q=449> ). We believe that the uncertainty may have two near- to medium-term impacts on global trade. The first, given that trade uncertainty has risen, and the administration has not disclosed how it will proceed after the Sec. 122 tariffs expire in 150 days, this may impact medium- to longer-term freight planning. The second, more near-term impact, will be countries that now have a lower effective tariff rate may pick up exports into the U.S. We note that this may take time to show up in the data, particularly for freight out of mainland China as manufacturing and shipping pick back up following a later than usual LNY.

We do note that the ongoing geopolitical conflicts could impact ocean and air rates higher, but we need to dissect the driver of rate increases from capacity constraints and surcharges versus import demand and the impact of tariffs.
