AXIA Energia (AXIA3.SA): Conversion of ISA Energia ordinary shares + asset swap announced; Positive 
	
20 March 2026 | 10:30AM BRT | Research | Equity 	
	
	
On March 19, after market close, Axia announced the conversion of ISA common shares into preferred shares <https://publishing.gs.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Fapi.mziq.com%2Fmzfilemanager%2Fv2%2Fd%2Fabb77a17-3348-4bc7-849a-154998e06ca3%2F5ed48276-c4b6-4802-e71b-1aa05a6895d1%3Forigin=2/1/0100019d0b73ba1d-a52f9070-03f4-41b2-aa31-b8026d385b5c-000000/D-7fLmXkf_9iTqlEKzsfRpBDwShKr917YlVcUNyEiZg=449> , as well as the unwinding of transmission equity interests <https://publishing.gs.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Fapi.mziq.com%2Fmzfilemanager%2Fv2%2Fd%2Fabb77a17-3348-4bc7-849a-154998e06ca3%2F01bc0ba6-9f56-e77f-bf22-d31be0f43efc%3Forigin=2/1/0100019d0b73ba1d-a52f9070-03f4-41b2-aa31-b8026d385b5c-000000/d3KRaDYALIHQBNW1d8uT8ziGByVkb4n9d5mOKy_f0Eo=449>  with ISA Energia.

We see both announcements as positive for Axia. In our view, the 19.8mn ordinary shares conversion into preferred shares is another step towards its stated intention for a sale of the company's participation in ISA Energia (as ordinary shares are illiquid), which at today market close prices would amount to ~BRL 4bn in proceeds for Axia if sold (~2% of market cap for reference).

Regarding the unwinding of equity interests in the special purpose entities Madeira and Garanhuns transmission lines, we also view the move as another positive step towards corporate structure simplification and monetization of illiquid assets (consistent with the company's strategic plan). The eventual conclusion of the transaction will lead to a BRL 1.2bn receivable + the consolidation of the Garanhuns transmission asset, contributing an additional BRL 134mn to Axia's consolidated EBITDA and BRL 43mn to net debt.

All in all, we see both announcements as positive for Axia. We reiterate our Buy rating on the company, backed by its strong combination of a relatively uncontracted power generation portfolio in a context of rising power prices, further asset optimization initiatives (such as the ones discussed in this report) and a clear path to higher dividends ahead.
