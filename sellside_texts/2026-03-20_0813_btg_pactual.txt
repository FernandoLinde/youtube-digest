Simplifying corporate structures

Axia and ISA Energia announced a transmission asset swap aimed at simplifying both companies’ corporate structures. Axia will transfer to ISA Energia its 49% stake in the Madeira transmission line (R$761mn in RAP for 100%), while ISA Energia will transfer to Axia its 51% stake in the Garanhuns transmission line (R$158mn in RAP for 100%) and will pay Axia R$1.17bn in cash. After the transaction, Axia will consolidate 100% of Garanhuns, while ISA Energia will consolidate 100% of Madeira.

Fairly priced asset swap

We ran our calculations and reached an NPV of R$1.8bn for Axia’s 49% stake in Madeira and R$500mn for ISA Energia’s 51% stake in Garanhuns, which, combined with the R$1.17bn cash payment, totals R$1.7bn. As such, we view the transaction as fairly priced, with a neutral impact on valuation for both companies.

Positive transaction

We view the transaction as positive for both companies. In addition to simplifying their corporate structures, the transaction will provide liquidity for Axia and is in line with its strategy of divesting minority stakes. For ISA Energia, it should help slightly reduce leverage, from 3.63x ND/EBITDA in Dec-25 to 3.49x ND/EBITDA with the consolidation of Madeira.

Conversion of ISAE3 shares into ISAE4 shares

Alongside the asset swap, Axia approved the conversion of 19.76mn ISAE ON shares into PN shares at a 1:1 ratio. After the conversion, Axia’s stake in ISA Energia (21.6% in Dec-25) will be concentrated in PN shares, where free float and liquidity are higher. The conversion also aligns with Axia’s strategy of optimizing minority participations and might represent the first step of additional divestment of Axia’s participation in ISAE.
