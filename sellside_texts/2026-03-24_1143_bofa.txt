Let me add my favorite charts…some ideas I like…structural long US AI (MLUSAI basket) + tactical long S&P Energy vs SPX and short private equity (PLP), short ACWX, or EZU or EEM, EEM downside protection for June…pls refer to the Survey to your conclusions…

 

Still no capitulation… our broadest measure of FMS sentiment, based on cash levels, equity allocation, and global growth expectations, dropped sharply from 8.2 to 5.6, a 6-month low, but still well above the Apr'25 "Liberation Day" low of 1.8…



 

Cash levels jumped sharply from 3.4% last month (and 3.2% record low in January) to 4.3% in March, the biggest jump since March 2020. Cash levels are an input into the BofA Bull & Bear Indicator, which is down to 8.5 from 8.7. Bull & Bear Indicator triggered a contrarian "sell" signal in December.



 

Survey shows the first hit of a second wave higher in inflation expectations…net 45% expect higher global CPI in the next 12 months (up from 9% a month ago)…



 

 

Concerns over AI hyperscalers overspending on capex have eased in the past few weeks…I’m looking forward to selling Oracle 5y CDS…



 

Asked what is the most likely source of a systemic credit event, FMS investors said private equity/private credit (63%), for the 8th month in a row…still worth shorting PSP vs SPX…



 

 

Asked about Brent oil by year end, just 11% expect the price to be over $90/bbl (Brent currently $102/bbl)…I would keep adding S&P Energy and Brazil’s PRIO3…



 

Note 28% now expect a "blue wave" (Democratic House & Democratic Senate), up from 11% just two months ago…



 

IS DXY bottom in? I like expressing this view through EEM downside protection for June….I’ve been pithing this for the last weeks…



 

FMS investors were 34% net overweight commodities, the most since Apr'22…



 

Another reason why I keep reinforcing EEM downside protection…a region that has historically failed to post ROE above its cost of capital, and has succeeded in underperforming global equities…



 

I’ve been mentioning since end of last year, that I’d be adding long US equities through MAG7 or our AI basket (MLUSAI) vs short EEM or ACWX…positioning might be a tailwind…



 

No big wash out in global equities yet…



 

The fact that we’re going through a tech revolution and that positioning is far from euphoric levels, is mind-blowing…my constructive view on US AI names is structural…



 

 

The first energy overweight since May'24. Current reading is 0.3 stdev below its long-term average…long S&P Energy vs SPX is a tactical trade only…
