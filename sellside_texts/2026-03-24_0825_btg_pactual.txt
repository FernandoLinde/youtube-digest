Cutting estimates following weaker-than-expected 2025 performance

SMU’s recent results have been constrained by sluggish revenue growth, primarily reflecting continued weakness in the C&C segment. As a result, operating performance in 2H25 fell short of our expectations. Given the lower earnings base in 2025, we are reducing our 2026 EBITDA estimate by 8%. Our forecast implies an EBITDA margin of 8.1%, at the lower end of management’s 8.0–8.5% guidance range, reflecting our cautious stance following recent execution challenges. Even so, we still expect a 40bp margin expansion in 2026, followed by a further 20bp improvement in 2027.

We lower our target price to CLP$164 per share from CLP$180, implying a 16x 2027 forward P/E multiple. Our Neutral rating remains unchanged.

Improving setup into 2026

Comparable figures will be easier following the weak 2025 performance, when sales declined 2.5%. Management has indicated a stronger start to the year at Unimarc. Selling area growth accelerated to 3.5% in 2025. Gross margins have remained stable over the past three quarters, supported by a more disciplined promotional strategy. In addition, the company implemented further efficiency measures in 1Q26, the drag from format migration should ease, and our top-down view on Chile has become more favorable.

Net/net: cautious stance maintained

Part of the recent weakness reflects management’s decision to prioritize profitability over volume, a strategy that has resulted in market share losses but should be supportive of medium-term returns. However, despite an improving macro backdrop, we forecast only 3.9% revenue growth in 2026, as competitive pressures remain intense and the expected economic recovery is modest. Overall, earnings visibility remains limited, and it is not yet clear that SMU has a sustainable competitive advantage relative to peers. We would look for a clearer inflection in revenue trends before adopting a more constructive view on the stock.
