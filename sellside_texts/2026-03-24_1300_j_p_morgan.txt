What Are Investors Reading? 


If you like our research, consider your 5 stars vote for J.P. Morgan on the LatAm II Extel survey. Our team is featured in the following categories: Equity Strategy, Brazil Research, Mexico Research, North Andean Research, Argentina Research, Chile Research, ESG, Thematic and Economics. 

Below are the most-read strategy reports of the past few months. There are two main interests: 

*	Flows: To us, it is the biggest story for EM and LatAm since the Fed started to cut interest rates in September 2025. The question we ask ourselves every single day is on whether EM flows are to resume, even if at a more discreet pace, once oil prices find a footing. EM saw US$8 billion of outflows since the conflict started. On the other hand, Brazil has seen inflows of US$1 billion since then, which is incredible from a historical point of view. 
*	Argentina: Interest in Argentina reached a new peak with the congressional elections last year and since then, thematic reports on the country have been top of mind. 

The last two reports that were the most read are about Brazil and Mexico: 

*	Brazil and oil: We think Brazil (and LatAm for that matter) are clear relative winners of the current geopolitical crisis. The region is away from the conflict, is a net oil exporter (ex Chile and Peru), and Brazil still has the ongoing easing cycle and the optionality offered by the October elections. 
*	Mexico and USMCA: The USMCA renegotiation is the most important trigger for Mexico this year and the early start of conversations has made us more hopeful of a positive outcome by mid-year.
