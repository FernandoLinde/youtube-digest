YDUQS - Putting things into perspective

YDUQS hosted an NDR with a more cautious tone on intake, reflecting a softer cycle in its undergraduate segment. Still, the company highlighted relative outperformance vs. industry trends, with consolidated intake down ~7%. Retention remains strong, supporting stable revenues in 1Q. Margins should face some mix pressure, but improving credit quality (PDA) and limited regulatory impact should help offset this. For 2026, management expects free cash flow to be at least in line with 2025, with upside supported by lower non-recurring expenses, better financial results, and lower DIS penetration, despite lower PraValer inflows. Premium segments (medicine and IBMEC) remain key growth drivers. We maintain our Buy rating, supported by an attractive ~18% 2026 FCF yield.

Brazil Retail and Internet - Agentic commerce – when intent becomes the new storefront

We hosted an event today with Gleidys Salvanha, General Director of Retail for Google, and Natacha Litvinov, Head of Shopping and Payments, and in this report we take the opportunity to discuss agentic commerce (we have extensively written and studied the topic in the past quarters, and believe that this should increasingly become a recurring topic in the discussions about the sector). Agentic commerce represents the most meaningful evolution of digital discovery since the rise of mobile, shifting the paradigm from search → browse → compare → transact toward intent → decision → execution. In our view, this is not an immediate substitute for traditional search, but a structural overlay that gradually absorbs high-intent queries.

Brazil Retail & Consumer Goods - When the ball stops the mall: The dual impact of the World Cup on Brazilian retail

Historical World Cup cycles provide consistent evidence of meaningful sales acceleration in sporting goods, but the often-quoted +20–40% uplift is best understood as a triangulation across multiple datasets rather than a single disclosed figure. In Brazil 2014, industry data from IBGE and Abrasce showed sporting goods and apparel categories growing ~+18–25% y/y in June–July, versus low single-digit growth in preceding months. Importantly, this uplift is concentrated in a 6–8 week window, implying a temporary but highly profitable demand surge.

Helbor - Big (positive) land sale to curb leverage

Helbor announced today an MoU with Cyrela for the acquisition of a stake in an SPE (HESA 159), aiming to develop a low-income project (likely under the Vivaz brand) in the “Semp Toshiba” land plot. In addition, the company also announced that Cyrela is acquiring ~19k CEPACs (Certificados de Potencial Adicional de Construção), in cash, held by the SPE. We believe the move sends a positive message: Helbor seems committed to taking steps to curb leverage and increase asset turnover. However, immediate deleverage effects from this transaction should be limited, since: (i) we estimate that the company will collect ~R$40mn in cash; plus (ii) another ~R$250mn during the project, which may be launched in 2027. We see this deal as positive (cash collection represents 16% of Helbor’s net debt); BUY.

Brazilian Utilities - 2026's first transmission auction

On March 27th, ANEEL will hold the first transmission auction of the year. The auction will feature 5 blocks with a total estimated capex of R$3.4bn, 274 km of transmission lines, 825 MVA of substations, five synchronous compensators, and a maximum RAP of R$580mn (17.3% RAP/Capex). The projects are spread across 11 states - RJ, SP, MG, PR, SC, RN, CE, BA, SE, MT and PA - with energization deadlines of up to 60 months. Block 3 is the largest (capex of R$1.4bn; RAP of R$232mn), followed by Block 5 (capex of R$1.0bn; RAP of R$186mn), which together account for 71% of total capex.

Petrobras & Fuel Distribution - Federal Government Introduces Guidelines for the Diesel Subvention Program

Yesterday, the federal government announced the decree 12.883/2026 that basically establishes that Petrobras needs to sell diesel at the current price (R$3.65/liter) to gain access to the R$0.32/liter diesel subvention from the federal government. Therefore, the reference price for Petrobras is R$3.97/liter. For importers and private refineries, the reference prices will be based on an import parity price (methodology to be defined by ANP).

Axia & ISA Energia - Fairly priced transmission assets swap

Axia and ISA Energia announced a transmission asset swap aimed at simplifying both companies’ corporate structures. Axia will transfer to ISA Energia its 49% stake in the Madeira transmission line (R$761mn in RAP for 100%), while ISA Energia will transfer to Axia its 51% stake in the Garanhuns transmission line (R$158mn in RAP for 100%) and will pay Axia R$1.17bn in cash. Mater Dei - Better-than-expected Q4 results

Mater Dei delivered a strong Q4, with results above expectations and margin expansion as the key highlight. Net revenue grew 16% y/y (+3% vs. BTGe), while EBITDA rose 64% y/y (+5% vs. BTGe), with margin reaching 22.6% (+6.7pp y/y). Performance was driven by higher ticket (+9% y/y), reflecting better mix and increased complexity, alongside strong cost dilution. Cash generation remained solid, with FCF of R$73mn, while net debt increased q/q mainly due to shareholder remuneration. Even so, leverage improved to 1.7x ND/EBITDA. Overall, results reinforce the recovery thesis, with consistent margin gains, improving execution and earnings momentum, while the stock still trades at attractive levels (~10x 2026 earnings).

BTG Pactual Container Shipping Compass - Pause after the surge

This week, the SCFI index remained broadly stable, declining a modest 0.2% w/w to 1,707 points. This was driven by strength in China–U.S. routes, with Shanghai–LA and Shanghai–NY increasing 4% and 7% w/w, respectively, partially offset by softer performance on Europe-related trades, including Shanghai–Rotterdam (+1%) and Rotterdam–NY (-2%). By contrast, the CCFI—which captures both spot and contract rates—rose 5% w/w to 1,121 points, pointing to underlying strength in contracted pricing despite flat spot dynamics. Similarly, Drewry’s WCI global spot rate for a 40-foot container increased 2% w/w to US$2,172/FEU, with trade dynamics broadly mirroring those observed in the SCFI—strength in Shanghai–NY (+8%) and Shanghai–LA (+4%), partially offset by a 2% decline in Rotterdam–NY.
