*	Petrobras (OW): It’s All About Governance – Brazilian Government Sets Diesel Reference Price Framework Our take: the announced reference price still implies a 30% discount to parity, but the price increase is still a decision of Petrobras. Federal government published Decree 12,883/2026 on the determination of the diesel reference price for subsidies in Brazil (see Diesel Relief Measures and 12% Export Tax for additional context). The decree assigns to the Ministry of Mines and Energy the responsibility for setting the current reference price, with ANP defining the methodology for subsequent updates. It also establishes differentiated methodologies that, in Petrobras’s case, set the current R$3.70/l price as the cap for receiving the R$0.32/l subsidy announced last week. We calculate that, at this reference price, even factoring in the subsidy, the discount to import parity remains approximately 30%. We note that Petrobras operates under a formal pricing policy and governance framework. While external pressures may persist, we expect the company to continue making decisions consistent with these guidelines.
