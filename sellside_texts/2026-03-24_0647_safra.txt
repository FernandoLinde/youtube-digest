HIGHLIGHTS

 

*         Diesel prices at the pump rose 6.6% in a single week and have now surged 20.6% since the start of the Middle East war, reaching a national average of BRL 7.35/liter, according to ANP data. The acceleration reflects not only Petrobras' (PETR4; OP; TP BRL 43,00) 11.6% refinery price hike but also the pass-through of higher costs from private refiners and importers, whose replacement costs are already priced at elevated market rates. Regional averages range from BRL 7.30 in the Northeast to BRL 7.46 in the North. Gasoline rose 2.9% in the same period, accumulating 5.9% since the war began. The government has ramped up enforcement operations, resulting in fines against over 52 distributors and 1,192 fuel stations to date. (Ag.Infra <https://agenciainfra.com/blog/anp-diesel-nas-bombas-sobe-66-em-uma-semana-e-206-desde-o-inicio-da-guerra/> ) 

*         MP 1.343/2026 expands minimum freight floor enforcement through mandatory CIOT registration and real-time blocking of below-floor operations. The measure integrates CIOT with MDF-e and links data across ANTT, Receita Federal and state/municipal tax authorities, creating a fully traceable audit trail for every road freight operation. Fines range from BRL 1 million to BRL 10 million per infraction for contracting companies, with liability extended to shareholders and affiliated entities. The MP has an initial 60-day validity and requires Congressional approval to become permanent. (Tecnologística <https://www.tecnologistica.com.br/noticias/transporte-rodoviario/21146/mp-amplia-fiscalizacao-do-piso-minimo-do-frete-e-preve-multas-de-ate-r-10-milhoes/> ) 

*         Escalation of the Iran conflict threatens semiconductor and battery supply chains, with potential knock-on effects for the automotive and EV industries in Brazil. The region accounts for a significant share of global petrochemical feedstocks used in chip manufacturing, and logistics disruptions are already affecting delivery lead times for key components. (Autodata <https://www.autodata.com.br/noticias/2026/03/20/extensao-do-conflito-no-ira-ameaca-fornecimento-de-chips-e-baterias/101140/> )

* Rumo (RAIL3; OP; BRL TP 19.00)is a lateral player to agricultural growth. In 2024, corn and soybeans accounted for 26% and 28% of the company’s total shipped volumes, respectively. As global grain prices rise, producers are incentivized to export more, driving demand for rail logistics services.
