Peru metals and mining - Mining concession draft proposal to face defeat

Peru’s Energy and Mines Commission has advanced a comprehensive overhaul of the General Mining Law, approving a substitute text by a margin of 11 votes to one, with three abstentions. This legislative package merges a dozen draft bills originating from diverse political factions and introduces three principal reforms: an escalation of concession fees, the imposition of more rigorous penalties, and a substantial reduction in the period allowed before a concession expires due to non-initiation of production. The amendments are particularly consequential for large-scale concessionaires, stipulating an increase in annual fees from US$3/ha to US$12/ha for holdings exceeding 100,000 ha – a threshold that encompasses nearly all major established mining firms.

Brazilian Steels - What is going on?

The Brazilian steel sector faces persistent challenges despite anticipated price hikes. (I) Antidumping effects remain limited; imports surged 34% y/y, pushing penetration to 28%-33%. Domestic sales fell 3%, demand softened, and margins are near crisis levels below 10%. (II) Contract resets reduced automotive prices; flat steel commands a 14% premium over China, limiting hikes; construction shows resilience. (III) Global oversupply, notably China’s, depresses prices; absent broad tariffs, profitability will stay pressured, with gradual improvement expected only later in the year.

ESG Research - Brazil's truck fleet renewal: a new attempt to reduce emissions

Renewing Brazil's aging truck fleet offers a pathway to emission reduction and improved safety yet faces systemic limits. (I) In 2024, Brazil’s fleet of 2.3 million trucks averaged over 12 years old, contributing to high emissions and inefficiency. (II) The Move Brasil program allocates R$10 billion in financing to replace older trucks, requiring dismantling of retired vehicles; initial uptake reached R$5 billion in approved credits. (III) Broader emission cuts necessitate diversifying transport modes, enhancing rail and water freight, intermodal logistics, and biofuel adoption to address structural dependencies beyond fleet renewal.
