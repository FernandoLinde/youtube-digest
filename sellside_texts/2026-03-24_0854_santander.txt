Brazil Strategy - Markets Reprice a Fatter Right Tail – Aline Cardoso

Report Link <https://pierpont.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12840416&ad=4077682110&h=hI_48kT&sig=MyksAaGyG5oKa7hVi1U5BZERa6W&v=2&url=https://santanderresearch.bluematrix.com/links2/html/ad7fd396-8f37-4225-81b1-3e62f05773e2> 

*	Our central scenario still leans toward a temporary disruption of energy markets rather than a lengthy structural dislocation. We believe the Strait of Hormuz will reopen eventually, but it is hard to say when. Even in a benign path where the conflict de-escalates soon, oil flows could take months to normalize, in our view. Infrastructure damage, security concerns, and the simple realization of how fragile that choke point is could keep a persistent geopolitical premium embedded in oil prices.
*	In practical terms, that likely means oil settling into a higher range than pre-conflict assumptions.
*	For Brazil, this creates a tricky mix: supportive for terms of trade, but with second-order inflation effects that could complicate the easing cycle.
*	The tail risks are no longer theoretical.
*	We still see our base case as the most likely outcome, but the range of possible scenarios around it has clearly widened — and the tails are getting heavier.
*	As more oil and gas infrastructure is hit, the probability of a prolonged supply shock is creeping higher. That opens the door to a less friendly macro backdrop: slower global growth, potentially sticky inflation, and difficult policy choices.
*	And as the probability creeps higher, we believe equities could start to struggle in response.

*	The financial system reported 12 million incidents of fraud in 2025. According to a survey by Quod, a data intelligence company, the Brazilian financial system registered 12 million incidents of fraud in 2025, a 67% YoY increase. The study shows that social engineering is the main entry point for scams, responsible for 44% of cases. The peak in reported fraud incidents occurred at the end of the year, with 2 million occurrences in October and 1.8 million in November and December together. These figures represent a notable 200% YoY increase, which, according to Quod, is related to Resolution 501 of the Brazil Central Bank (BCB) going into effect in October, which expanded the volume of information shared via Rufra (Quod's Unified Fraud Registry). (See Valor Econômico’s March 19 article, “Sistema financeiro registra 12 milhões de indícios de fraude em 2025, aponta Quod”.)
*	Digital money will coexist with Pix. Despite the advance of tokenization, stablecoins, and cryptocurrencies, the prevailing assessment among experts is that there will not be a single dominant model, but a coexistence between different forms of money, such as stablecoins, tokenized deposits, central bank digital currencies (CBDCs), and already established systems like Pix. Thus, the use of digital assets is likely to expand beyond the crypto universe and reach activities such as payments, credit, foreign exchange, and settlement of financial transactions. Based on these developments, B3 is already working to expand its operations in this field, with initiatives involving stablecoins and other digital assets, as well as regulated products that provide access to this market. (See Valor Investe’s March 19 article, “Dinheiro digital não vai substituir o Pix — vai conviver com ele”.)
