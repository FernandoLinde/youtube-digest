JEF VOL | Macro and Mag7

 

Macro

Heading into the second half of March investors appear well hedged with the put/call ratio near 1-yr highs and elevated short interest across ETFs. As such, the desk favors supply chain related hedges because even if tensions cool, damage to supply chains will take time to amend. Strikes on Qatar’s Ras Laffan wiped out ~17% of the regions LNG capacity. For context, Qatar produces roughly 20% of global LNG supply. Furthermore, the doubling of fuel oil threatens the transportation supply. Companies that produce and transport LNG stand to benefit from these bottlenecks. Chemical producers will also likely be disrupted by higher oil and naphtha prices as well as shipping delays. Fortunately for DOW, they’re relatively better positioned versus peers because its inputs are tied to US light feedstocks.
