Finnair acquires up to 46 E2s; potential ~US$4bn order at list prices

This morning, Embraer announced that the Finnish airline Finnair is acquiring up to 46 E195-E2 jets (18 firm orders + 16 options + 12 purchase rights). In our view, this order comes as part of Finnair’s broader strategy to renew its E190 and A319 fleet. In terms of list prices, we estimate the value of the order at ~US$4bn at list prices (~13% of consolidated backlog and ~28% of commercial backlog as of Q4). We also believe that this order for newer (and more efficient) generation aircraft signals an initial de-risking of ongoing demand concerns stemming from the impact of Middle East tensions on overall airline economics and capacity growth plans. We welcome this order as another validation of the E2 platform, something that was particularly important last year. Embraer still holds a large backlog that secures deliveries for the years to come, and we thus reiterate our BUY.

More about the customer and the order

Finnair is one of the most established airlines in the Nordic region, with a total ASK of 9.5bn and annual revenues of €790mn as of 2025. As of Q4, the company operated aircraft from three OEMs: Airbus for both narrow- and wide-body operations, and ATR and Embraer for regional aviation under the Norra brand. Additionally, during its capital markets update last year (here <https://ir-cdn.inderes.com/assets/finnair/calendar-event-attachments/ac6ff8c9-7e7f-4580-b0a0-ae4c5d0110d0/finnair-capital-markets-update-2025.pdf> ), the company highlighted that its E190s were already 17 years old and undergoing refurbishment, and that marginal fleet renewal will focus on narrow-body and regional aircraft, with €2.0–2.5bn in investments expected from 2026 to 2029, resulting in fleet growth of 10–20% over the period. Altogether, we believe that the E2 will serve as a more efficient aircraft (35% lower fuel costs compared to the previous generation) within an already well-established operation in Europe maintained by the airline.

This event reinforces (even more) Embraer's presence in Scandinavian airspace

This event also highlights Embraer’s E2's strong product fit and value proposition for intracontinental flying in Europe. Additionally, we believe that it also marks Embraer’s strong presence in Scandinavian airspace, which also saw relevant orders last year, including (i) SAS for E2s (we wrote about it here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/112060> ) and (ii) Sweden for KC-390s (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/115327> ). We also note that, as of 4Q25, both SAS and Finnair already operate previous-generation Embraer aircraft, with fleets of 16 and 12 jets, respectively.

We believe marginal orders are key de-riskers of ongoing global tensions

Over the previous weeks, EMBJ’s shares have been underperforming, mainly due tothe market pricing in the impact of a higher-for-longer oil scenario on overall airline economics. Accordingly, we view this order as evidence that (i) amid this scenario, marginal orders will likely go to more fuel-efficient, newer-generation aircraft and (ii) to aircraft with shorter lead times, in order to fulfill ST capacity additions in core and non-disrupted markets, unlike what has been happening in the Middle East. Nonetheless, we also note that the impact of higher oil prices is marginal when it comes to medium-term earnings momentum, as the company had already secured a US$32bn backlog by 4Q25, although we do expect some limited pressure on margins given the inflationary effects of such dynamics. Altogether, we believe that if the main concern regarding Embraer was related to marginal orders, the Finnair order acts as a major de-risking factor. Lastly, in our view, the 9x EV/EBITDA26 valuation also looks undemanding compared to aviation industry peers.
