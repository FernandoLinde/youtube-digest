Yesterday, Finance Minister Haddad announced that he has stepped down to run for Governor of Sao Paulo. There was already speculation that this would occur, so it likely does not come as a surprise to the market. Taking Haddad’s place is his 41-year-old deputy, Dario Durigan. Valor notes this morning that while Durigan’s name had already been put forward by Haddad, this was the first time Lula has publicly confirmed the choice himself.

 

With global interest rates and inflation at the forefront of investor attention in the current scenario, our economists in Mexico think the Central Bank will pause <https://pierpont.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12829453&ad=4069322460&h=cci8D1r&sig=NfgC5WfHaIkkbFh50shkt86PQfi&v=2&url=https://santandercibinsights.com/report/5000ac02-7f7d-42a1-9991-794ab5122bdf>  on rates when they meet next Thursday. Inflation remains too lofty and they are not seeing enough improvement within the core indicators to warrant a cut when combined with the geopolitical factors. For the time being, we stick to our view that the CB will cut in June and August.

 

Abraham Fuentes is changing recommendations in Mexico transportation, <https://pierpont.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12829453&ad=4069322460&h=q_GnXnl&sig=E7px84AYBryNk63EqgYZ5ZVSbCW&v=2&url=https://santandercibinsights.com/report/f959d161-87dc-42e1-a69c-480bdd219121> upgrading GAP and downgrading Volaris. For GAP, we upgrade to outperform we are seeing an opportunity on valuation (new TP M$472/sh + 4-5% DY) as we believe near-term headwinds in Guadalajara and Puerto Vallarta (due to cartel related issues in Feb) as well as a soft Mexico international segment are priced in, and that the market is missing a quicker than expected recovery in Jamaica following Hurricane Melissa. On Volaris, we still believe that the demand outlook should be constructive ahead, but the global oil volatility has pushed jet-fuel prices higher and with higher fuel-cost assumptions we now lack upside in the model (TP M$13/sh) and downgrade to Neutral.

 

Aline Cardoso & Andres Soto have conducted a thematic analysis on the impact from Gen Z’s reconfiguration of the consumer universe <https://pierpont.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12829453&ad=4069322460&h=pw250ss&sig=KZX0H505XVNfN4e8OGGlG0LGhiM&v=2&url=https://santandercibinsights.com/report/d196f4e4-950d-468a-9ee4-85b55949b94c> . The key findings are that Gen-Z views wellness as habitual, not aspirational, and there’s a firm preference for mobile-first ecosystems and a “bring it to me” mindset. However, Aline and Andres emphasize recent reporting that, counterintuitively, suggests that in LatAm physical retail isn’t dead, as visiting the mall with friends has become a retro social event. To play this thematic the obvious candidates are Mercado Libre and Yape (Credicorp). But Aline and Andres suggest looking also at names like Smart Fit and PetzCobasi that are tied to recurring, identity-driven tastes. And Multiplan, Iguatemi, Fibra Uno and Parque Arauco which are leveraged to the reconfiguration of physical retail to the social and experiential vibe. In Mexico, we see Alsea as the clearest beneficiary.

 

Lucas Barbosa’s done some serious number crunching on Brazil’s power consumption needs and the implications for grid renewal <https://pierpont.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12829453&ad=4069322460&h=mMO7PDR&sig=A0nonwSSOPWK7Vyyou881Fc2mmY&v=2&url=https://santandercibinsights.com/report/f57dfeed-97e1-48c6-918f-a1bf87ca531f> . The aim is to figure out what this means for WEG given their sizeable investments in their power transmission & distribution business which carries much higher margin potential. He’s come away convinced that WEG’s investment plan could generate net revenue of up to R$186bn through 2035.
