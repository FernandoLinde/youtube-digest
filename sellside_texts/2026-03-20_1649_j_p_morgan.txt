Today, we hosted Mr. Carlos Aguiar (Head of Sales ) and Mr. Filipi Campos (Head of Market Insights) from Close-Up International in Brazil. The main takeaways from the call were: 1) the Brazilian pharma market remains structurally robust, growing double-digits over the past decade, with large chains representing ~50% of sales; 2) GLP-1 products should continue to print solid growth trends, with the expiration of the patent for semaglutide (today in Brazil) being a relevant trigger to significantly increase the TAM; and 3) Tirzepatide (Mounjaro) represents today ~65% of GLP-1 sales but its growth is constrained by supply. Please find the detailed notes from the webinar below. The presentation used by Close-Up International can be provided upon request. 

*	Bottom Line. Brazilian pharma retail remains structurally robust (~US$48bn market, double-digit growth), but GLP-1 therapies have become the central driver of incremental value creation. With ~4.7% of market value and growing rapidly, the category is expanding the market, reshaping prescription behavior, and increasing channel concentration. Supply constraints, high pricing, and limited distribution suggest the market is still underpenetrated. The next phase should be driven by broader access, increased competition, and price normalization, supporting continued strong growth while gradually expanding beyond the current high-income patient base. 
*	Brazil Pharma Retail: Category Structure and Demand Profile. The Brazilian pharma retail market figures among the top globally, and has delivered consistent low double-digit value growth over the past decade. This growth is notably price/mix-driven, with unit expansion running at only ~4–5% p.a., implying strong premiumization. The market remains heavily skewed toward branded products, with prescription drugs accounting for ~50% of total value (rising to >55% when including GLP-1s), non-medication categories at ~30%, and OTC at ~14%. The out-of-pocket nature of the system continues to shape purchasing behavior, reinforcing brand preference but also embedding price sensitivity at the margin. 
*	Channel Concentration and Route-to-Market Dynamics. Brazil has a highly fragmented physical footprint (~100k pharmacies), but revenue is concentrated: large chains account for ~48% of total sales while representing only ~15% of stores. This concentration drives superior sales productivity and makes these chains the primary access point for premium therapies, such as GLP-1.
