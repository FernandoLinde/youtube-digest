AMZN, WMT, & COST command ~44c of every incremental dollar of retail sales, and the group continues to outperform pre-Covid levels. E-commerce is a bright spot, while the rest of retail has yet to drive meaningful share gain. 

5 takeaways from the Q4'25 Retail Funnel:

1. AMZN, WMT, & COST command ~44c of every incremental dollar of retail sales. These three retailers continue to be market share leaders in the world of retail, with AMZN's Q4'25 share of incremental retail sales reaching ~24.6%, WMT's share reaching ~11%, and COST's share reaching ~7.9% (Exhibit 1). This combined level of share is consistent with the Q3'25 level and marks a modest acceleration vs. 1H25. With the rest of retail struggling to gain share, the Funnel tells the story of retail with the big getting bigger at an accelerating pace. Stepping back, we believe this level of combined share is healthy and encouraging, as it marks a step-up from the ~40%-42% range in 2019 & 2020 prior to the volatility induced by the Covid-19 pandemic (Exhibit 2).

2. WMT's incremental share decelerated slightly vs. Q3'25 but remains strong. WMT's ~11.0% share of incremental retail sales represents a ~25 bps deceleration vs. Q3'25. That said, share trends can be somewhat choppy on a quarter-to-quarter basis and the Q4'25 level is above the 1H25 rate of ~10.6%. This degree of share gain is the second highest in retail and reflects continued momentum within WMT's flywheel.

3. Within e-commerce, WMT and AMZN continue to lead the industry. We estimate AMZN's and WMT's share of incremental e-commerce sales reached ~86% and ~38% in 2025, respectively (Exhibit 3). That said, while we typically track share using incremental Adjusted Census E-commerce Retail Sales, we and our MS Internet team think the 2025 data may not reflect the underlying growth rate of industry e-commerce sales. After adjusting for Motor Vehicle & Parts and well as Miscellaneous & Gasoline Stations, the Census E-Commerce Retail Sales data suggests the industry grew +4.9% in 2025, which is a meaningful deceleration vs. the ~7%-8% range in 2024, and implies industry e-commerce penetration was +20 bps y/y (vs. the ~85 bps gain in 2024, per Exhibit 4). In contrast, AMZN and WMT (which combined comprise >50% of the adjusted total on an absolute basis) continue to demonstrate strong e-commerce growth, with WMT's e-commerce sales growing ~25.7% in 2025 and AMZN's e-commerce GMV growing ~9.8%. We think this divergence is too significant and believe industry e-commerce sales grew closer to ~HSD% in 2025. Assuming ~8.5% industry growth, this indicates AMZN & WMT incremental share reached ~49%/~22% in 2025, respectively, which represents durable momentum against an increasingly competitive backdrop.

4. Dollar Stores continued to take share in Q4'25. Combined share of DG/DLTR/FIVE/OLLI reached ~2.9% in Q4'25, which is near multi-year highs (Exhibit 5). As we have written previously in our 2026 Outlook <https://ms.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12838626&ad=4076304963&h=ROrBhJr&sig=UcZ40uIVjlDVglsptD9QJhCH2de&v=2&url=https://ny.matrix.ms.com/eqr/article/webapp/c14e5ba2-dac5-11f0-a3ae-2aac6342f563?ch%3Drp%26sch%3Dar%23/section%3D12> , we believe the group benefitted from widespread store closures and retailer underperformance in 2025, driven by macro pressures and intensified competition from large scale players such as Walmart and Amazon. Looking ahead to '26, we expect dollar store momentum to slow as these benefits fade, while Big Lots and Temu show signs of recovery and other retailers improve execution against easier comparisons.

5. Among the other mega caps, TGT & HD are struggling to gain share while LOW is slowly building back to positive territory. For TGT and HD, share of incremental retail sales declined by ~20 bps/~30 bps to -1.1%/-0.3%, respectively, in Q4'25. The lack of momentum is consistent with broader retail, as consumer sentiment remains weak, the housing market has yet to turn, and increasing cost pressures are limiting consumer discretionary demand. Within this backdrop, LOW is executing well and is diverging slightly from the group with Q4'25 incremental retail sales reaching positive territory (+0.5%) and 2025 share improving to ~flat vs. LSD% declines in 2023 & 2024.
