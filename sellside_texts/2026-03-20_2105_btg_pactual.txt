Impressive 23% y/y FX-neutral growth

Bemobi delivered another strong set of results. Net revenue reached R$199.2mn, up 20.5% y/y (or 23% FX-neutral) and 6% q/q, coming 4% above our estimate. The strong top-line performance was primarily driven by Payments (+50% y/y, +17% q/q; 8% above BTGe) and SaaS (+23% y/y, +8% q/q; 2% above BTGe). Microfinance revenues totaled R$20.2mn, up 6% y/y, supported by solid momentum in credit scoring, while Subscription revenues declined 7% y/y (or were roughly flat FX-neutral), coming in better than feared. 4Q marks the seventh consecutive quarter of double-digit y/y growth and the fifth with high-teens to low-twenties top-line expansion. Profitability also remained strong, with EBITDA margin at 33.1%, broadly stable y/y. Adjusted EBITDA reached R$65.9mn (+20% y/y), 4% above our estimate. Finally, adjusted net income (excluding mark-to-market effects from swap contracts) totaled R$61mn, up 31% y/y and well above our R$45mn estimate, mainly driven by lower taxes.

Payments +50% y/y and +17% q/q; Telcel onboarded

The 50% y/y growth in the Payments division was driven by TPV growth of 36% y/y and a take-rate expansion from 2.5% in 4Q24 to 2.8% in 4Q25. The main highlights in the quarter were the Education segment and Sabesp. We estimate that Education and Utilities already account for nearly 40% of total TPV (R$3.1bn), with Telecom representing the remainder. In revenue terms, however, the mix is more balanced, given the higher take-rates in these segments. Bemobi also announced a new flagship client in 4Q25: Telcel, the leading telecom operator in Mexico and the company’s first client in the country. Unlike Brazil, Bemobi does not have exclusivity in Telcel’s channels and will initially operate in only one channel. That said, the opportunity is significant, as Telcel has ~85mn subscribers, of which ~69mn are prepaid.

Growth, profitability and shareholder return. A rare mix breed in tech

Bemobi combines double-digit growth, profitability, and shareholder return — a rare mix in Brazil’s tech landscape. On a relative basis, it also looks inexpensive under every angle (at just 11x 2026E earnings). Today’s results reinforce our bullishness on the name.
