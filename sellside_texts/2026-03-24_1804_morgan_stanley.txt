AI: Mapping the AI Ecosystem

As the compute investment cycle accelerates, and capital remains constrained, new financing solutions are emerging. We update and expand our illustration of capital flows in the AI ecosystem to reflect the latest announced investments and incorporate new types of financing structures. 
Key takeaways	 

*	A significant portion of AI funding remains circular and tied to long-dated compute purchase agreements, enabling scaling but creating interdependencies. 
*	The new funding often covers only a portion of the total compute commitment with the remainder of the contract reliant on revenue growth or new fund raising. 
*	Customers are funding suppliers with warrants tied to long‑term commitments. These deals improve demand visibility but can overstate headline pricing. 
*	Emerging hyperscaler guarantees/backstops support data center construction, but off‑balance‑sheet treatment can obscure the guarantor’s total economic exposure. 

In our recent note AI: Capital Intensity Enters a New Era <https://ny.matrix.ms.com/eqr/article/webapp/e71dda64-f2ea-11f0-b5dd-981530a7f3f3?ch=rp&sch=ar> , we highlighted that the AI investment cycle is reaching unprecedented levels of capital intensity, with hyperscaler capex set to surpass prior dot-com peaks and account for an outsized share of total investment. The widespread use of finance leases pushes reinvestment levels higher. We estimate AI-related investment could reach ~50% of total large cap capex in the next few years.

At the same time, capital remains constrained outside of hyperscalers, prompting the rise of novel financing structuresacross the ecosystem. Nvidia and the hyperscalers are enabling infrastructure scaling through equity investments in their customers and other arrangements, such as long-term purchase commitments and financial backstops. The accounting for these innovative structures is often highly complex and warrants more disclosure so investors can better understand interdependencies and distribution of risk.

In response to investor interest, we have updated our previous circularity mapping <https://ny.matrix.ms.com/eqr/article/webapp/17344bee-a3bc-11f0-bdb2-a16197e2f2f1?ch=rp&sch=ar>  for OpenAI (Exhibit 1). We use this ecosystem for illustrative purposes, but similar ecosystems exist across other major AI ecosystem players, public and private.
