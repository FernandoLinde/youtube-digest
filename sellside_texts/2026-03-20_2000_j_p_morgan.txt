Cyrela reported 4Q25 results (link here <https://jpmorgan.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12827673&ad=4067901420&h=SjLBUaX&sig=Rt9zX06m6chxzIdpH0p3t9GbtvB&v=2&url=https://www.rad.cvm.gov.br/ENET/frmExibirArquivoIPEExterno.aspx?NumeroProtocoloEntrega%3D1492614> ) on Mar. 19th after the market close. The company had a strong quarter with top-line beating consensus expectations by 16% at R$3.2bn (+29% yoy) leading to a net income of R$682mn, +17% vs consensus, at a record high. Cyrela’s stake in Cury ended the quarter at ~15.0% vs ~15.9% in 3Q due to Cury’s YE follow-on offering. We see CYRE trading at 6.3x P/E 26e and 1.2x P/BV vs. EZTC at 7.3x/0.76x. 

*	Our take. We expect a positive reaction in tomorrow’s trading session given the strong bottom line being 17% above consensus (even without any CURY divestment), driven by solid top-line growth and implying an annualized ROE of 21.1%, +1.2pp yoy and +7.5pp vs JPMe. Moreover, the stock is down 14% in the past month vs IBOV at -4%. 
*	Main positives. i) Top-line was at R$3.2bn, +29% yoy and +41% vs JPMe / +16% vs consensus; ii)  SG&A / Revenues was at 12.8%, +0.4pp yoy, but 2.7pp below JPMe; iii) Net Financial Results were positive at R$66mn, 20% better than JPMe and compared with R$16mn a year ago; iv) Taxes were positive in R$2mn vs our call of -R$54mn (we look for color during tomorrow’s call); and v) Net income was at R$682mn, +37% yoy and implying a net margin of 21.1%, +1.2pp yoy and +0.6pp vs JPMe and consensus. 
*	Main negatives. i) Adj. gross margin was at 33.7%, +0.4pp yoy but -1.7pp vs our expectations, being impacted by sales of the studios and store of Vista Milano project (~1.8pp impact, implying recurring reported gross margin at 34.0%); ii) Minority payout was substantial at 18.8% vs our call of 15.4% and 15.6% a year ago; and iii) CYRE had an operational cash burn of R$38mn in the quarter vs generation of R$172mn in 3Q and R$61mn a year ago. 
*	Operational figures. As previously released, net presales (% CYRE) in 4Q were at R$2.50bn (-31% yoy) with concluded inventory sales at R$195mn (100% stake) vs R$185mn in 3Q25 and R$216mn year ago. Launches (% CYRE) were at ~R$3.44bn (-31% yoy), with the low-income segment representing ~35% of 4Q launches. Quarterly consolidated sales speed was at 17.2% (-14.2pp yoy due to tough comps) and inventory sales speed was at 10.8%, -1.7pp yoy and -0.2pp qoq, marking the worst figure since 1Q22. Inventories at market value totaled R$12.0bn (% CYRE), representing ~15 months of sales.
