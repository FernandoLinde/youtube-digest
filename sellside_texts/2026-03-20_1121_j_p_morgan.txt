*	Latin America (Guilherme Mendes): The short-term outlook for airlines in Latin America remains closely tied to the evolution of jet fuel prices, which account for nearly 40% of cash costs. Given the fluid nature of the current environment, we suggest caution – please refer to our joint report <https://jpmorgan.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12833905&ad=4072413686&h=auypl7p&sig=6LyTBcGrFNh1A7AiKW70SkU041U&v=2&isig=N8QlnDgu1aVFYeRsyxdU2M1oqvx&url=https://markets.jpmorgan.com/research/email/scx/jbobqvt5/GPS-5230955-0/56a71452-5e56-40a8-87e7-a62a06e73c47> with the Credit Research team, which includes a sensitivity analysis on jet fuel prices. For the companies under our coverage in the Latin America Airlines cluster, we favor LTM (OW) and CPA (OW) due to a combination of discounted valuation relative to historical levels, lighter balance sheets than peers, and consistent free cash flow generation and dividend payments. Both companies have consistently delivered robust operational results over the past few quarters. We recently initiated coverage on Aeromexico (OW), highlighting its positioning as the only full-service carrier in Mexico and leading carrier at Mexico City Airport (MEX), with higher exposure to international routes. However, we acknowledge that growth is likely to be more moderate than peers’, justifying a cheaper valuation. For VLRS (N), we acknowledge that the outlook for the aviation environment in Mexico should remain challenging in 2026.
