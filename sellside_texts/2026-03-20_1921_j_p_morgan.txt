ANTAD’s February SSS grew 1.1% y/y, down from 2.0% in January 2026. Total sales growth of +3.1% y/y in February decelerated 220bps from January’s pace. Within segments, Specialized Stores were the only segment to accelerate versus January, while Department Stores remained in negative territory and Supermarkets, though soft, performed in line with our 1Q26 expectations, which anticipate an acceleration compared to 4Q25. Overall, February’s reading supports our view that discretionary consumption is the most at risk as consumers face higher price elasticity (here <https://jpmorgan.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12827605&ad=4067865694&h=GgUhbLv&sig=3racKq6QTWhYoq9P4FzlBUgV3SQ&v=2&isig=TaOKx1BkX7tLMfcPq9APyYuld7d&url=https://markets.jpmorgan.com/research/email/scx/78e64nlv/GPS-5137865-0/38751416-2b49-46db-a676-f2d882e6a092> ) than in 2025, while the pace of recovery in basic consumption remains soft. With this in mind, we reiterate our preference for OW-rated TBBB, which remains our top sector pick given its good value proposition in the current environment with solid mature SSS underscoring solid volume and wallet share gain. 

*	Supermarkets SSS posted headline growth of +1.1% y/y in February, following 2.9% in January, with no calendar effect for the month. If momentum in February holds for March, it would imply SSS for 1Q could be at 2.8%. This compares to ~5.0% on average expected for our covered Co avg (ex. Soriana), a 2.5pp outperformance vs ANTAD — in line with YTD outperformance. 
*	Department Stores trends remained in negative territory for the third month in a row with headline and calendar-adjusted figures -0.7%/-4.1%, respectively. The calendar-adjusted figure represents an improving trend vs. January’s -6.2%, yet is still in negative territory. This supports our view that discretionary spending is at risk considering the loss of cyclical income and savings depletion during 2025. 
*	Specialized Stores posted 3.4% y/y growth in February, up from 3.2% in January but down from 3.8% in 4Q25. While the correlation is loose, this compares to the 3.3% we currently forecast for FEMSA in 1Q26E, which implies an acceleration vs. 2.1% in 4Q25.
