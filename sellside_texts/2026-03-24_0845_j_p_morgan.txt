*	Construction halt in SP creates uncertainties in the sector – Valor Econômico <https://jpmorgan.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12842598&ad=4079269188&h=IFuNE-6&sig=SdclSL8K4P2KgqoczlW9alEg6L6&v=2&url=https://valor.globo.com/empresas/noticia/2026/03/23/suspensao-de-alvaras-gera-incertezas-em-obras.ghtml> . The halt in construction permits in SP is creating uncertainties in the sector, with Secovi-SP saying that the entire city is being affected, while some lawyers are arguing that it should only cover areas impacted by the change in the zoning law from Jan 2024. Moreover, Eztec signaled a potential need to replace around R$664mn in its 2026 launches PSV guidance (currently at R$2.5-3.0bn) if the measure persists. Retail and hospital groups warn of delayed investments and jobs. However, already issued permits remain valid and reviews continue without document issuance. The injunction was maintained and awaits judgment with no set date, with an appeal also to the Supreme Court. 
*	Cyrela signs a MoU to be partner of Helbor in a MCMV ptoject with a sales PSV of R$1.5bn – Press Release <https://jpmorgan.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12842598&ad=4079269188&h=OKPlXqj&sig=M2asrELu5gsb3Zv9BpkN18acyK0&v=2&url=https://www.rad.cvm.gov.br/ENET/frmExibirArquivoIPEExterno.aspx?NumeroProtocoloEntrega%3D1492914>  and Metro Quadrado <https://jpmorgan.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12842598&ad=4079269188&h=MCfMG2E&sig=FHqpTw4YDyRg6HPsiOfXfBCkBuo&v=2&url=https://metroquadrado.com/residencial/cyrela-sera-socia-da-helbor-em-projeto-do-mcmv-com-vgv-de-r-15-bi/> . Helbor announced the signing of a MoU with Cyrela (OW-rated) for the potential acquisition by Cyrela of a stake in HESA 159, a company in which Helbor holds participation, to jointly develop a MCMV housing project on the Semp Toshiba land (~26k m²) in São Paulo – representing a sales PSV of R$1.5bn. Helbor will retain a 30% minority stake in HESA 159, with Cyrela acquiring the remaining 70|%, with the possibility to acquire ~19k CEPACs currently held by HESA. The transaction is subject to customary conditions, including approval by CADE (Brazil's antitrust authority). 
*	High-end residential boom transforms São Paulo’s North zone – O Estado de São Paulo <https://jpmorgan.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12842598&ad=4079269188&h=4ZbfQOG&sig=Mz7HxQ3mkMxdjzwTX7rW7aazzF5&v=2&url=https://www.estadao.com.br/economia/negocios/zona-norte-de-sp-vive-boom-imobiliario-com-imoveis-de-alto-padrao-entenda-motivos/> . Since 2020, São Paulo’s North Region has seen a surge in high-end residential launches, with 55,423 apartments delivered or in the pipeline at an average price of R$9.2k/m², and luxury units reaching up to R$16k/m². Major projects like Cidade Center Norte (R$2bn investment, VGV R$7.7bn, over 6,000 units) and premium launches by AW Realty and Mitre are reshaping the region, driven by post-pandemic demand, local land bank strategies, and new commercial amenities. Urban infrastructure upgrades are planned to support this growth, including new tunnels, viaducts, and the extension of Avenida Braz Leme, aiming to benefit up to 800k residents.

Shopping Malls & Properties 

*	Goodman transforms 60k m2 chemical plant into warehouse, with 90% leased for Mercado Livre – Metro Quadrado <https://jpmorgan.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12842598&ad=4079269188&h=3jGM1WS&sig=Qx8Gioc6TQSe7fbqRVrMbQElvKr&v=2&url=https://metroquadrado.com/comercial/goodman-transforma-industria-quimica-em-galpao-e-atrai-mercado-livre/> . Goodman converted a former Rhodia chemical plant site in Santo André into a 60k m² logistics warehouse, securing Mercado Livre (MELI US; N-rated by Marcelo Santos) as a tenant for 90% of the space while the remaining 10% was pre-leased to a logistics operator. The project was delivered in November and required a R$360mn in investment. The redevelopment addresses scarce land within São Paulo and aligns with Mercado Livre’s expansion of last-mile operations after a recent lease in Raio 60. The site’s 150k m² required environmental decontamination and integration of two heritage buildings, now serving as a cafeteria, meeting rooms, a café, and a Rhodia museum. 
*	Portuguese company wants to attract up to R$650mn to develop open shopping centers in Brazil – Valor Econômico <https://jpmorgan.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12842598&ad=4079269188&h=61ZB9UR&sig=LQJZ8q4QBCmEQBgbqaL87H6sW5u&v=2&url=https://valor.globo.com/empresas/noticia/2026/03/21/empresa-portuguesa-quer-atrair-ate-r-650-milhoes-para-construir-shoppings-abertos-no-brasil.ghtml> . The Portuguese company RetaildMind is seeking to raise R$600-650mn over five years to develop open shopping centers in Brazil—commercial spaces larger than strip malls but smaller than traditional malls. The CEO, Vitor Rocha, said that the company is looking for land in Greater São Paulo for 15-25k/m2 spaces, preferably on city edges with easy car access and free parking. 
*	São Paulo’s “Times Square” project spurs debate on outdoor advertising law and urban revitalization – O Estado de São Paulo <https://jpmorgan.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12842598&ad=4079269188&h=yRJ4GOI&sig=K74jRwJghqMrsFLaBPXtD89C2nM&v=2&url=https://www.estadao.com.br/sao-paulo/lei-cidade-limpa-sp-times-square-o-que-nova-york-londres-seul-ensinam/> . The approval of large LED advertising panels (300–1000m²) at the iconic Ipiranga and São João intersection in São Paulo, with R$36mn in panel investment and R$6mn in private urban improvements over three years, reignites debate on the Lei Cidade Limpa. While international cases like New York, London, and Osaka show that well-regulated advertising zones can boost commerce and tourism, Seoul’s experience warns of excessive flexibility leading to complaints and visual pollution. 
*	Hotel chains are expanding into Brazil's agribusiness – Valor Econômico <https://jpmorgan.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12842598&ad=4079269188&h=R7D4y8F&sig=4Jn48IZ43ncI8DI2K8TFxdxdvWV&v=2&url=https://valor.globo.com/empresas/noticia/2026/03/23/redes-de-hoteis-avancam-para-cidades-do-agronegocio.ghtml> . Hotel chains are expanding into Brazil's agribusiness cities, with regions in the Center-West, South, and Southeast becoming construction hotspots. The French chain Accor currently operates 30 hotels with ~4k rooms in agribusiness cities, up from 18 hotels a decade ago, while Atlantica has grown 80% to ~7k rooms across 48 properties. The sector benefits from local entrepreneur financing and development bank support amid high interest rates, with Atlantica's pipeline including 16 hotels representing R$1bn in investment through 2031. 
*	Artessence plans to open a Parfums de Marly boutique in São Paulo in 2026 – Valor Econômico <https://jpmorgan.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12842598&ad=4079269188&h=srRTBzi&sig=9bf2Qwp7lCkA8KGVQm7GpQSLdvx&v=2&url=https://valor.globo.com/empresas/noticia/2026/03/22/artessence-planeja-butique-da-parfums-de-marly-em-sao-paulo.ghtml?> . Parfums de Marly and Initio, two brands of France’s Artessence (controlled by Advent), are accelerating in Brazil with shop-in-shops at Cidade Matarazzo next month and a standalone Parfums de Marly boutique planned for 2026 in São Paulo. The group targets US$1bn in sales in the fiscal year of 2025 and expanded production from 400k bottles in 2021 to 4mn in 2026, with Parfums de Marly growing ~40% annually and up 75% in Brazil, now leading the country’s niche segment with 47 points of sale.

Morgan Research <https://jpmorgan.email.streetcontxt.net/platform/al?a=12842598&ad=4079269188&h=0VNX3Wq&sig=LTX7BcGbW96EgXMbIPm5KEtdJai&v=2&isig=7ZoMBYWCdXd95EROMXLL0nwAHCj&url=https://markets.jpmorgan.com/research/email/scx/-2m27b1s/GPS-5242016-0/0dd60d7f-0134-40e6-a798-e41fab52e1b9> . Core CPI (excluding controlled and food components) ticked up from 4.2% oya in January to 4.6% in February. We viewed this increase as driven by one-offs, a conclusion reinforced by our supply and demand decomposition. The uptick was concentrated in items for which the decomposition was not attributed to either demand or supply shocks.
