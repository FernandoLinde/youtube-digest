2) LT thematic pieces - some interesting Food for Thought reports

Also during last year and the first months of 2026, we delved deeper into several thematic topics to better assess long-term implications across multiple investment theses. Below are some of our key thematic and longer-term publications so far this over the previous years:

Localiza – Is it really too big to grow? (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118125> ): This week, we published a report discussing Localiza’s growth outlook from multiple angles. Our key conclusion is that, with some normalization in vehicle supply, used car prices, and cost of capital, there is room for a selective resumption of growth, provided it is accompanied by discipline and returns.

WEG – The easy growth theory (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118355> ): This quarter, we also explored WEG’s opportunity in the motors segment. The company is operating in a competitive landscape where incumbents such as Siemens, Nidec, and General Electric are facing idiosyncratic challenges, which we believe open a meaningful avenue for organic growth in WEG’s EEI business over the medium to long term.

GPS – A Master Class on GPS (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/117572> ): In January, we hosted GPS’s COO, Gustavo Otto, to discuss key organizational and sector dynamics. Key takeaways: (i) organic growth has been driven by internal governance improvements since 2022; (ii) growth constraints relate more to leadership development than scale; (iii) the GRSA acquisition enhanced execution capabilities; and (iv) tax reform should structurally support outsourcing. Overall, we believe the market underestimates GPS’s structural growth and medium-term upside.

Motiva/Ecorodovias – The rise of an industry (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118653> ): During March, we hosted our traditional Toll Roads Day in Rio de Janeiro, with the participation of Motiva, Ecorodovias, and other relevant private operators such as EPR, Arteris, and Vinci. Across meetings, while discussing company-specific trends, a key takeaway was how operators in the sector have been able to generate shareholder value through regulatory mechanisms.

Localiza – Quick update on the digitalization journey (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/117443> ): In this report, we analyzed Localiza’s digitalization agenda, with a focus on the FAST product and its fully digital customer journey. We believe the product should see exponential growth in the coming years, further supporting margin expansion and overall efficiency gains.

WEG – Some basics on BESS and energy storage (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/117412> ): In January, we published a primer on BESS, following similar reports last year on motors, synchronous condensers, and alternators. In the report, we analyzed the product itself, alongside WEG’s market positioning and opportunities. The key takeaway is that BESS represents another growth lever amid rising energy consumption and generation, ultimately driving demand for grid-enabling and resilience solutions.

Embraer – New plane, why not? (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118021> ): This report framed the debate around a potential new aircraft at Embraer through a long-term strategic lens, arguing that product renewal is a structural necessity in aviation rather than an option. With mature, cash-generative platforms and improved balance sheet flexibility, Embraer appeared better positioned to manage development risk, while the cost of inaction could gradually erode its competitive relevance.

Localiza – The three ripple effects of lower interest rates (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/115148> ): Lower interest rates are a key driver for Localiza, with three main transmission channels: higher equity value, reduced financing costs, and a more supportive automotive cycle. As a capital-intensive business, the company is particularly leveraged to rates, although a meaningful portion of fixed-cost debt could delay the full pass-through of lower funding costs.

WEG – Why have margins become structurally higher? (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/116199> ): WEG’s structurally higher margins reflect three main drivers: a richer mix toward long-cycle projects, stronger profitability in international operations, and a broader global margin expansion trend across electrical equipment peers. While some short-term volatility may still emerge from FX and tariff pressures, the report argues that the new margin level is supported by more durable business fundamentals.

Embraer – Embraer’s Indian runway (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/116967> ): India is a largely underappreciated growth vector for Embraer, with opportunities spanning commercial, defense, and executive aviation. Strong structural demand—supported by traffic growth, infrastructure expansion, and rising income levels—combined with a still-limited regional footprint, suggesting meaningful upside potential not yet fully reflected in expectations.

Localiza – Why is depreciation so high? (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/112356> ): In this report, we discuss the structural drivers behind higher depreciation at Localiza, highlighting factors such as the widening gap between new and used car prices, higher fleet age, and a less favorable sales channel mix. While part of the pressure is cyclical, normalization would likely depend on improved affordability, faster fleet rotation, and a more balanced resale strategy.

Motiva – The Five Verticals of Value (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/112455> ): In this note, we highlight five key value drivers behind Motiva’s performance, including improved capital allocation, active portfolio optimization, and a strengthened governance structure. Additional support comes from a more favorable regulatory backdrop and ongoing efficiency initiatives, which together suggest potential for sustained value creation beyond interest rate dynamics.

WEG – The great library of WEG (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/115007> ): In this report, we compile a structured “library” of prior WEG research, organizing key topics such as products, long-term growth drivers, competitive dynamics, and M&A history into a single reference framework. The note serves as a fast-track guide to the investment case, helping contextualize the company’s strategic positioning and key debate areas.

3) ST thematic pieces - riding on the (many) storms

Additionally, we also provided insights and shorter-term research, primarily in response to broader market volatility and near-term trends. Below, we compile our most relevant notes on this front so far last year and this year:

Transportation and Capital Goods – AI-pocalypse or AI-vantage? (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118126> ): We analyzed risks and opportunities across each sub-sector within our Transportation and Capital Goods coverage, concluding that the infrastructure segment appears the most resilient, while also offering opportunities for efficiency gains in both opex and capex.

Embraer – The 2026 Airshow Tracker (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/117411> ): Last year was particularly active for Embraer in terms of order intake. Alongside this backlog build-up, we observed a meaningful correlation between share price performance and news flow related to incremental orders. While we expect this dynamic to persist into 2026, it should moderate as investor focus shifts toward execution. Overall, 2026 is shaping up to be a busy year for airshows, potentially supporting further order announcements. 

GPS – A few thoughts on labor regulation changes (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118351> ): In this report, we assessed the potential impacts of changes in working arrangements (including the end of the 6x1 schedule and a reduction in weekly hours) on GPS, the largest labor outsourcing player in Brazil. Overall, we believe these changes may require some contract renegotiations, but remain manageable in our view.

Airlines – An oil shock playbook (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118643> ): In a higher oil price environment, concerns around airlines tend to increase. In this report, we analyzed airline exposure, hedging strategies, and fuel-related economics, concluding that CPA and LTM appear to be the most resilient under the current scenario.

Marcopolo – Minor changes, major gains (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/112549> ): In this report, we analyze regulatory changes in Rio’s urban bus system, highlighting a shift toward performance-based remuneration that should structurally improve sector economics. We believe these changes can support fleet renewal and volume growth, benefiting industry dynamics, with Marcopolo positioned to capture gains despite not being the market leader in the segment.

Rumo – The playbook of Rumo (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/114719> ): Rumo’s strategy appears focused on optimizing pricing, efficiency, and capital allocation despite a softer freight environment. While lower pricing visibility has increased perceived risk, management reinforced execution discipline, operational gains, and capacity expansion, supporting the view that long-term fundamentals remain intact.

Embraer – Is the Gripen fighter jet the new growth frontier? (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/116399> ): The Gripen contract with Colombia reinforced the potential for Embraer’s Gavião Peixoto facility to serve as a key production hub, supporting Saab’s growing backlog and future international demand. In parallel, the KC-390’s certification for aerial refueling with the Gripen expanded cross-selling opportunities, suggesting an additional layer of medium-term growth for Embraer’s defense segment.

WEG – Study Session – Readings from ABB Capital Markets Day (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/116542> ): Using ABB’s Capital Markets Day as a benchmark, key industry trends—such as electrification demand, margin expansion, and product premiumization—appear to reinforce WEG’s strategic positioning and long-term growth drivers. While ABB’s greater localization and scale highlight some gaps, overall dynamics suggest a supportive backdrop for WEG’s continued execution.

Rumo – Re-rating dreams (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/112328> ): In this note we argued that a re-rating in Rumo would likely come less from near-term earnings upgrades and more from lower perceived cost of capital. The key triggers highlighted were clearer deleveraging, reduced leverage concerns at Cosan, and greater confidence that EBITDA growth would outpace the capex cycle.

GPS – Study Session – Culture Speaks Louder (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/116751> ): We explored GPS’s corporate culture as a key long-term value driver, highlighting traits such as decentralization, meritocracy, and a strong service-oriented mindset. These elements are seen as critical to execution, M&A integration, and sustained growth, reinforcing the company’s competitive positioning beyond traditional financial metrics.

Motiva – A known departure (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/116460> ): Motiva’s airport sale to ASUR largely confirmed previously known terms, making the announcement more of a formal milestone than a surprise for the market. Still, the transaction marked an important step in portfolio simplification, with proceeds expected to support deleveraging, lower cost of capital, and a sharper focus on roads and urban mobility.

4) Model Updates - the key adjustments post-Q4

Lastly, we also published several model updates. In these reports, we refreshed our estimates and incorporated more recent trends and assumptions across our coverage, aiming to consolidate the most relevant themes and drivers observed during the inter-earnings period. Below, we summarize our most relevant model updates so far this year.

LATAM Airlines – 10 thousand feet... (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/117595> ): The report argues that LTM has moved past its “takeoff” phase in 2025—when strong macro tailwinds and execution drove growth and margins—and is now in a more stable “cruising” stage, with solid (though no longer accelerating) performance. It highlights continued growth, healthy margins, and shareholder returns as key supports for maintaining a BUY rating, despite recent stock re-rating driven by prior earnings upgrades. While no near-term downturn is expected, the report emphasizes that the airline sector remains inherently cyclical.

Motiva – The simpler, the better (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/117841> ): The report reviews Motiva’s evolution from a highway operator during Brazil’s privatization wave into a more diversified—but complex—infrastructure company, and highlights its recent strategic overhaul, including management changes and portfolio simplification. It emphasizes the ongoing asset recycling and shift back to a more focused, “pure” infrastructure play, with Q4 marking a milestone as the first results without the airport business. Overall, the report supports the transformation, citing improved governance, capital discipline, and execution, while noting attractive valuation with an ~12% real IRR and potential for tighter spreads vs. government bonds.

WEG – The red pill or the blue pill? (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/117859> ): The report acknowledges a weak Q4 and softer short-term growth outlook for WEG—pressured further by a stronger BRL—making FY26 look challenging. However, it expresses strong confidence in the company’s long-term growth, driven by expansion into higher-value segments (high-voltage, drives/automation) and structural opportunities like U.S. grid and data center investments. Despite the demanding valuation and near-term risks, the report maintains a BUY rating, favoring long-term upside over short-term headwinds.

Marcopolo – One notch up (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118080> ): The report revisits the downgrade to Neutral after Q3, which was driven by short-term slowdown concerns and a perceived deceleration in medium-term growth. Q4 results were better than expected and helped ease near-term concerns, but signs of slowdown persist into early FY26, with weaker mix and planned stoppages still impacting performance. While there may be a short-term catch-up trade given the attractive valuation and improved sentiment, the longer-term outlook remains unchanged, supporting a Neutral stance.

Localiza – One step at a time (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118123> ): The report highlights that Localiza’s Q4 showed improving margins and strong used-car sales trends, supporting the case for upward earnings revisions. However, management adopted a cautious tone, reinforced by non-linear used-car price dynamics (e.g., FIPE data), signaling that the recovery may not be smooth. The report agrees with this conservative stance, emphasizing that disciplined execution should support a steady ramp-up in the used-car business and help manage expectations.

Rumo – Are we done giving bad news? (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118356> ): The report discusses Rumo’s pricing reset, which weighed on Q3 and Q4 results and should continue to impact Q1, likely marking the final quarter of negative pricing adjustments. It takes a constructive view, arguing that the reset was necessary to recover volumes and improve commercial terms with trading houses. Additionally, better capex discipline and clearer strategic priorities are seen as key positives, potentially paving the way for a gradual improvement in investor sentiment.

GPS – A clockwork thesis (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118359> ): The report highlights GPS’s Q4 results and conference call as key for aligning market expectations on growth, margins, and sector trends. The company sustained ~10% organic growth, stabilized margins through normalized ramp-up costs and GRSA improvements, and signaled a return to M&A-driven growth while managing labor changes. Overall, GPS is seen as highly disciplined, delivering steady growth and margin gains, reinforcing its position as one of the report’s top cross-sector picks.

Embraer – Is it really different, or just a bit stranger? (here <https://www.btgpactual.com/research/report-view/118425> ): The report notes that Embraer’s stock has seen cautious investor reactions post-Q4, reflecting FY26 guidance and the company’s shift from backlog growth to execution and delivery. Despite a less “exciting” phase amid geopolitical and oil-price headwinds, fundamentals remain solid, with strong divisional performance, robust backlogs, and optional upside from the eVTOL platform. Trading at a discount to global aerospace peers, the report views the post-Q4 market reaction as exaggerated and sees an opportunity to revisit the investment case.
