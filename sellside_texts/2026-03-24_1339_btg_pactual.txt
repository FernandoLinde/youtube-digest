Please find below our Andean (Colombia and Peru). Chile and Argentina Weekly Recap

 

ARGENTINA

Data Releases

*	Supermarket sales decreased -1.2% y/y in real terms in January.
*	Shopping Center sales decreased -0.3% y/y in real terms in January.
*	Trade surplus was US$788mn in February.
*	Exports were US$5,962mn in February, down -2.1% y/y.  
*	Imports were US$5,174mn in February, down -11.8% y/y. 
*	Consumer confidence in March was down -5.3% m/m.
*	Fiscal Balance was ARS$144.4bn in February, was down from ARS$1,105.2bn last month.  
*	4Q25 GDP was up 2.1% y/y, below market expectations of 2.2%. FY25 GDP was up 4.37% y/y.

Corporate News

*	Banco Macro SA (BMA US): The company announced that they have entered into a stock purchase agreement for 100% of the outstanding capital stock and voting rights of Banco Sáenz S.A. The purchase price will be equivalent to: (i) an amount in Argentine Pesos equal to the shareholders’ equity of Banco Sáenz S.A., to be determined prior to closing; and (ii) US$2.0mn.
*	YPF SA (YPF US): The appeals court in New York suspended all the plaintiff’s requests until the key elements of the lawsuit get resolved in the case over the expropriation of YPF.
*	YPF SA (YPF US): Compañia Mega, a firm owned by YPF, DOW and Petrobras, applied for a RIGI project for a new investment of US$360mn in Vaca Muerta in the context of its 5-year plan of investing over US$600mn in Argentina.

Politics/Others

*	Banks: The central bank authorized banks to distribute up to 60% of the profits generated in 2025.

 

CHILE  

Data Releases

*	4Q25 GDP was up 1.7% y/y, 10bps below market expectations. FY25 GDP was up 2.5%.

Corporate News

*	CAP SA (CAP CI): The company agreed to invest ~US$20mn in Aclara Resources via a share subscription (C$2.83/share), increasing its stake to ~12.9% (from ~10.1%), with potential to reach up to 19.9% under the 2024 strategic alliance.
*	Falabella SA (FALAB CI): The newly elected board of directors unanimously appointed Fernando de Peña (former CEO of Mallplaza) as chairman, replacing Enrique Ostale.
*	Vina Concha y Toro SA (CONCHA CI): The company launched a share buyback offer that will remain open until April 6, offering to acquire up to 29.3mn shares at CLP925 per share through a book-building process (OFB).

Politics/Others

*	Weather: The International Research Institute (IRI) published its latest ENSO forecast seeing a 72% chance of El Niño in the May-July period (NOAA below 50%) and an 80% probability in the June-August window (vs just north of 60% from NOAA).
*	Copper/Investments: Freeport-McMoRan submitted a US$7.5bn expansion plan for its El Abra mine, aiming to quadruple output and add >300k MT/year by 2033.
*	Copper: BHP’s Escondida has advanced its new concentrator initiative, a capital project valued between US$4.4bn-5.9bn, into the environmental assessment system.

 

COLOMBIA

Data Releases

*	In March's survey of expectations of the Central Bank:

	*	Inflation expectations for December 2026 and 2027 are at 6.18% (vs 6.19% from the previous month) and 4.75% (vs 4.77% from the previous month).
	*	FX rate expectations for December 2026 and 2027 are at $3,800 (from $3,789 the previous month) and $3,865 (from $3,841 the previous month).
	*	Central Bank rate expectations for December 2026 are at 11.77% (from 11.66% the previous month).

*	Imports (CIF) increased 9.7% y/y to USD $5,903mn in January. Imports were up 10.1% in the LTM.
*	Trade deficit of USD $-1,329mn in January, compared to a deficit of USD $-1,279mn last year and a deficit of USD $-1,173mn last month.
*	Financial system results through January 2026 showed gross loan growth of 0.5% m/m, 9.3% y/y. Corporate, consumer and mortgage loans were up 0.5% m/m (7.1% y/y), up 0.5% m/m (7.9% y/y) and up 0.7% m/m (12.1% y/y), respectively. Past due loans of 3.90% were up 7 bps m/m but down -83 bps y/y.

Corporate News

*	Ecopetrol SA (EC US): The company plans to expand the Cartagena regasification project alongside Frontera Energy, increasing capacity from 126 to up to 400 mcf/d by 2026. 
*	Ecopetrol SA (EC US): The USO union warned it could call a strike if CEO Ricardo Roa remains in position, citing ongoing investigations that create uncertainty. The board is currently evaluating alternatives, including maintaining Roa, granting temporary leave, or requesting his resignation.
*	Ecopetrol SA (EC US): Petrobras announced a new gas discovery at the Copoazu-1 well in deepwater offshore Colombia, supporting the emergence of a new offshore gas province in the Caribbean. Petrobras operates the block with a 44.44% stake, alongside Ecopetrol (55.56%).
*	GeoPark Ltd (GPRK US): Grupo Gilinski increased its stake to 24.1% after purchasing more than 505k additional shares for about US$4.4mn, from the initial 20% reached after fully subscribing a primary placement earlier in March. Gilinski was allowed to increase its stake up to 32% as part of the PIPE agreement.
*	Almacenes Éxito (EXITO CB): The company announced that it reached an agreement local supermarket chain La Anónima to transfer the operation of 12 stores and one distribution center in Argentina. Éxito will retain ownership of the underlying properties and will therefore operate as a real estate company in the country, adding gross leasable area (GLA) to its portfolio.

Politics/Others

*	Politics: US federal prosecutors in NY have opened a preliminary criminal inquiry into alleged links between President Petro and narcotraffickers. Prosecutors are reportedly questioning traffickers about alleged contacts with Petro’s representatives, including claims that bribes were solicited in exchange for blocking extraditions to the US, as well as possible illegal financing of the 2022 campaign. 
*	Elections: Two recent polls by GAD3 and CNC show Iván Cepeda leading first-round voting intention, with 35.0% and 34.5%, respectively. In GAD3’s survey, Abelardo de la Espriella ranks second with 21.0%, followed by Paloma Valencia at 16.0%. In contrast, CNC places Paloma Valencia in second place with 22.2%, while Abelardo de la Espriella comes third with 15.4%. Both polls place Claudia López and Sergio Fajardo in fourth and fifth positions, each with less than 5% support. In runoff scenarios, both surveys show Iván Cepeda defeating Abelardo de la Espriella, 45.0% to 36.0% (GAD3) and 48.1% to 35.5% (CNC). By contrast, both polls point to a technical tie between Iván Cepeda and Paloma Valencia in a potential second-round matchup.
*	Pension Funds: The central bank warned that the draft decree allowing the transfer of COP25tn in pension savings from AFPs to Colpensiones would likely be illegal. The central bank also highlighted that the reform is partially suspended by the constitutional court and that the contributory pillar fund is not yet operational, reinforcing the need to maintain current transitional rules.
*	Ecuador: Presidents Daniel Noboa and Gustavo Petro agreed to resume negotiations within the Andean Community to contain a growing trade dispute that had raised concerns of a potential commercial escalation between Ecuador and Colombia. 
*	O&G/Utilities: Local media reports that the government’s outstanding debt to the energy and gas sector has risen to COP9.2tn (US$2.5bn), largely driven by unpaid subsidies, tariff option costs, and official obligations. 

 

PERU

Data Releases

*	Industrial production in January was down -11.3% m/m and -2.0% y/y.
*	Electricity production was up 4.3% y/y in January.

Corporate News

*	Cementos Pacasmayo SAA (CPAC US): The market regulator (SMV) launched an investigation into payments made to senior management in the context of the sale of the company. In its 2025 financial statements, the company disclosed payments to advisors and executives related to the purchase agreement between Inversiones ASPI and Holcim.

Politics/Others

*	Elections: The latest Datum poll shows Keiko Fujimori leading the presidential race with 11.9% voting intention, closely followed by Rafael Lòpez Aliaga (11.7%), further down is Alfonso López Chau (6.5%), Carlos Álvarez (5.0%), and Jorge Nieto (4.6%). Notably, 35.8% of respondents opted for a blank vote, “none,” or provided no response. 
*	Weather: The government extended the state of emergency by 60 days starting March 20, covering districts across eight regions, to strengthen local authorities’ capacity to respond to heavy rains. The measure reflects rising concerns around the El Niño weather phenomenon, which is expected to bring adverse conditions in the coming months. 
*	Politics: President Balcazar appointed Luis Arroyo as prime minister, the third cabinet chief named in a month.
